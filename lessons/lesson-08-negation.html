1. ～はずがない・～わけがない

Formation

（ナA・N + な／である）+ はずがない・わけがない

Book explanation

Both forms are strong. はずがない often follows an expectation based on facts or a logical calculation. わけがない can sound more forceful and personal, as if saying 'there is simply no way.'

English study note

Core meaning: Say that something cannot reasonably be true or happen, based on evidence, knowledge, or judgment. Compare～とは限らない only says exceptions are possible. ～はずがない and ～わけがない reject the possibility much more strongly. Common mistakeDo not place a negative clause before these forms unless you intend a double negative: 来ないはずがない means 'there is no way he will not come.'

Book examples

Book example

ちゃんとしたんだから、がはずがない。どうしたのかなあ。

He promised properly, so there is no way he will not come. I wonder what happened.

Why this works

The promise is strong evidence, so the speaker confidently rejects the possibility that he chose not to come.

Book example

あのがのはずはありません。でんですから。

There is no way that shop is closed today. I confirmed it by phone.

Why this works

The telephone confirmation provides objective grounds for the speaker's strong conclusion.

Book example

なのだからはずがない。がんばらなくては……。

It is a national examination, so there is no way it will be easy. I have to work hard...

Why this works

The nature of a national examination supports the forceful judgment expressed by はずがない.

Book example

こんなに、わたしにわけがないでしょう。

There is no way I could buy such a large house.

Why this works

わけがない rejects the possibility from the speaker's practical circumstances and judgment.

Book example

にためにいるのだ。がきびしくないわけがない。

We are practicing to win the match. There is no way the practice would not be demanding.

Why this works

The purpose of winning makes strict practice an unavoidable, strongly expected fact.

2. ～とは限らない

Formation

（ナA・N + だ）+ とは

Book explanation

This form weakens an overconfident rule or assumption. It often appears with words such as いつも, みんな, 必ず, だれでも, or 絶対, because those broad claims invite exceptions.

English study note

Core meaning: Say that something is not always true and that another possibility remains. Compare～わけがない says something is impossible. ～とは限らない does not choose one outcome; it only warns that the expected outcome is not guaranteed. Common mistakeA negative clause before the form reverses the surface meaning: 失敗しないとは限らない means failure is possible.

Book examples

Book example

このはからわれているが、がみんないるとは。

Although this song has been sung for a long time, it does not mean every Japanese person knows it.

Why this works

とは限らない limits the generalization that a famous old song must be known by everyone.

Book example

がものががいいとは。

Expensive things are not necessarily always good quality.

Why this works

The pattern directly rejects the universal link between high price and high quality.

Book example

にけがをしないとは。においたほうがいいですよ。

You might get injured while traveling. It is better to have insurance.

Why this works

The negative clause before とは限らない means the safe outcome is not guaranteed.

Book example

にあることがいつものこと（だ）とは。

What is written in a newspaper is not always true.

Why this works

The form warns that a commonly trusted source can still have exceptions.

3. ～わけではない・～というわけではない・～のではない

Formation

（ナA・N + な／である）+ わけではない／ + というわけではない／（ナA・N + な）+ のではない

Book explanation

The listener may reasonably infer one explanation from the situation. These forms say that inference is not the full truth, and the speaker often supplies a narrower or different explanation afterward.

English study note

Core meaning: Correct an assumption or deny only part of a statement rather than rejecting the whole situation. Compare～わけがない rejects possibility. ～わけではない rejects an interpretation. The single particle で changes the meaning completely. Common mistakeDo not translate every occurrence as a total 'not.' Look for the specific assumption being corrected and preserve any fact the speaker still accepts.

Book examples

Book example

をおしたままでしたが、いたわけではありません。

I kept your book for a long time, but it is not that I had forgotten it.

Why this works

わけではありません denies the likely explanation without denying that the book was kept too long.

Book example

いつでもにわけではありません。はメールでします。

It is not that I can answer the phone at any time. Please contact me by email.

Why this works

The pattern partially limits availability rather than saying the speaker can never answer.

Book example

このが（だ）というわけではないが、といっしょにができて。

It is not that I like this job, but I enjoy being able to work with him.

Why this works

というわけではない rejects the broad inference while preserving the more limited positive point.

Book example

するのではありません。をんです。

It is not that I am being transferred. I am leaving the company.

Why this works

のではありません directly corrects an assumed explanation and supplies the actual one.

Book example

A「いいね。でしょう。」B「これはんじゃないの。での。」

A: Nice hat. It must have been expensive. B: I did not buy this. I made it myself.

Why this works

じゃないの conversationally denies only the assumption that the hat was purchased.

4. ～ないことはない

Formation

V／イAくない／ナA・Nではない + ことはない

Book explanation

The double negative does not sound as positive as a direct statement. It often means 'I could,' 'it is not impossible,' or 'I cannot say it is not,' and a limitation normally follows.

English study note

Core meaning: Say that something is possible or somewhat true, while implying a reservation. CompareA direct positive sounds confident. ～ないことはない sounds hesitant, qualified, or reluctant and prepares the listener for a drawback. Common mistakeDo not use it when you intend an enthusiastic yes. Without an understood reservation, the double negative can sound evasive.

Book examples

Book example

ここからまでことはありませんが、かなりがかかりますよ。

It is not impossible to walk from here to the station, but it will take quite a while.

Why this works

The double negative gives a reluctant yes, immediately followed by a practical limitation.

Book example

こののカレーもおいしくないことはないが、わたしはもっとのがだ。

This shop's curry is not bad, but I prefer something spicier.

Why this works

The speaker weakly acknowledges that the curry is good before stating a preference.

Book example

のがでないことはないのですが、はほっとしています。

I cannot say I am not worried about the exam result, but now that it is over I feel relieved.

Why this works

The double negative admits some concern without making it the speaker's strongest feeling.

5. ～ことは～が、…

Formation

V・イA + ことは + V・イA + が／ナA・N + なことは + ナA・N + だが

Book explanation

The key word is repeated around ことは. The speaker grants the first point instead of arguing about it, then uses が or けれど to show why that fact does not settle the matter.

English study note

Core meaning: Acknowledge that something is true, then contrast it with a reservation, limit, or less important fact. Compare～ないことはない gives a weak positive through double negation. ～ことは～が directly concedes the repeated positive or descriptive predicate. Common mistakeRepeat the same predicate on both sides of ことは. Changing it produces a different structure and loses the deliberate concession.

Book examples

Book example

からのはことはんですが、がよくわかりませんでした。

I did read his letter, but I did not understand the meaning well.

Why this works

Repeating 読む around ことは concedes that the action happened, then が introduces the limitation.

Book example

わたしはことはが、はだめなんです。

I can swim, but I cannot manage long distances.

Why this works

The repeated potential verb grants basic ability while limiting its extent.

Book example

このはことはが、がてそうだ。

This book is expensive, but it has many photographs and looks enjoyable.

Why this works

The adjective is repeated to acknowledge the price before giving a balancing positive point.

Book example

をのはなことはだが、がでさを。

Raising children is certainly difficult, but their growth is enjoyable and makes you forget the hardship.

Why this works

The na-adjective phrase is repeated to concede the difficulty before the contrasting reward.
