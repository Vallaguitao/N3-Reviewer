Place two different subjects side by side

1. ～に対して

Core meaning: in contrast to; whereas; compared with

Formation

N ＋ に対して · V普通形／イA普通形／ナAな／Nな ＋ のに対して

Nuance and use

Two separate subjects with a visible difference: Osaka versus Tokyo, women versus men, or an older child versus a younger child.

Use this pattern when two clearly identified subjects show different facts, habits, or qualities. The first side establishes the comparison point; the clause after に対して supplies the contrasting side.

The contrast is usually objective and balanced. It works well in reports, explanations, and comparisons between places, groups, siblings, products, or statistics. In this lesson, focus on contrast; Nに対して can also mean “toward N” in other contexts.

Compare

～に対して normally compares two subjects. ～反面 reveals an opposite side of the same subject.

Common mistake

Do not use this contrast pattern when the sentence only adds another fact about one unchanged subject. Make both sides of the comparison clear.

Examples

Book example

きのうはではだったのに対して、はいいだった。

Yesterday it rained heavily in Osaka, whereas Tokyo had fine weather.

Why this works

The same day's weather is contrasted between two places.

Book example

うちのはがよく飲みに行くのに対して、はまっすぐ家に帰る。

In our section, the women often go out for drinks, whereas all the men go straight home.

Why this works

Two groups in the same section have different after-work habits.

Book example

が好きなに対して、は家の中で遊ぶことが好きだ。

In contrast to the older son, who likes playing outside, the younger son likes playing indoors.

Why this works

The brothers are separate subjects with opposite preferences.

は朝早く起きるのに対して、はいつも遅くまで寝ている。

My older brother gets up early, whereas my younger brother always sleeps late.

Why this works

The brothers' morning habits create a direct comparison.

A：この二つの町はどう違いますか。 B：の町はにぎやかなのに対して、山の町は静かです。

A: How are these two towns different? B: The seaside town is lively, whereas the mountain town is quiet.

Why this works

The answer states the two subjects and one contrasting quality.

今年のが増えたのに対して、は下がった。

Sales increased this year, whereas profit decreased.

Why this works

Two business measures move in opposite directions.

が得意な姉に対して、私はのほうが好きだ。

Unlike my older sister, who is good at science, I prefer literature.

Why this works

The siblings are contrasted through their academic strengths and preferences.

では人口が増えているのに対して、では減り続けている。

The population is increasing in urban areas, whereas it continues to decline in rural areas.

Why this works

The formal comparison sets two regions and opposite population trends side by side.

A：新しい機種は軽いですね。 B：ええ。が重かったのに対して、これは持ち運びやすいです。

A: The new model is light. B: Yes. The old model was heavy, whereas this one is easy to carry.

Why this works

The speaker compares two models using the same practical standard.

の調査では反対が多かったのに対して、今回はが半数を超えた。

In the previous survey, opposition was common, whereas this time support exceeded half.

Why this works

Two survey rounds are compared through opposing results.

Guided checks

姉は朝型なの（　）、私は夜型だ。

去年は赤字だったの（　）、今年は利益が出た。

都会（　）、この村は夜になるととても静かだ。

Recap: Choose ～に対して when the sentence needs this relationship: in contrast to; whereas; compared with .

Reveal the other side of the same thing

2. ～反面

Core meaning: on the other hand; while it is true that…

Formation

V普通形／イA普通形 ＋ 反面 · ナAな／ナAである／Nである ＋ 反面

Nuance and use

One subject with a benefit and a cost, a strength and a weakness, or two consequences pointing in different directions.

This pattern describes two opposed qualities or consequences of one subject. The first side is often positive and the second negative, but the order can be reversed. Together they give a balanced evaluation.

反面 is common in explanatory and written language. It asks the listener not to judge from only one side: freedom can bring uncertainty, convenience can weaken a skill, and extra time can reduce tension.

Compare

～反面 keeps the same subject and turns it over to show its reverse side. ～に対して normally changes to a second subject.

Common mistake

Do not use 反面 for two unrelated facts. The second clause should feel like the other face of the first.

Examples

Book example

のは面白いことが多い反面、も多い。

City life has many interesting things, but on the other hand it also brings a lot of stress.

Why this works

Interest and stress are two opposing sides of the same city life.

Book example

はな反面、何でも一人でやらなければならないので、だ。

Traveling alone is relaxing, but it is also inconvenient because you must do everything by yourself.

Why this works

Freedom and inconvenience are inseparable sides of solo travel.

Book example

仕事をな時間が増えた反面、もなくなってしまった。

After quitting my job, I gained more free time, but I also lost my sense of tension and purpose.

Why this works

The same decision produces one welcome and one unwelcome result.

この部屋は広い反面、にお金がかかる。

This room is spacious, but heating it is expensive.

Why this works

The room's size is both an advantage and the source of a cost.

A：在宅勤務は楽ですか。 B：がない反面、仕事と生活を分けにくいです。

A: Is working from home easy? B: There is no commute, but it is hard to separate work from life.

Why this works

The answer balances the same work arrangement's benefit and difficulty.

この薬はよく反面、強いが出ることがある。

This medicine works well, but it can also cause strong side effects.

Why this works

Effectiveness and side effects are opposing aspects of one medicine.

スマートフォンは便利な反面、使いすぎるとしにくくなる。

Smartphones are convenient, but overusing them can make concentration harder.

Why this works

The convenience and its possible cost belong to the same device.

を大きくすれば利益を増やせる反面、も高くなる。

Increasing the scale can raise profits, but it also raises risk.

Why this works

Expansion creates two linked but opposing business effects.

A：あの先生は厳しいですね。 B：厳しい反面、学生のことをよく考えています。

A: That teacher is strict. B: Yes, but that teacher also thinks carefully about the students.

Why this works

The response adds a favorable reverse side to the teacher's strictness.

によって作業が速くなった反面、機械を管理できる人材が必要になった。

Automation made the work faster, but it also created a need for people who can manage the machines.

Why this works

The same technological change produces efficiency and a new staffing demand.

Guided checks

この仕事は自由な（　）、収入が安定しない。

便利になった（　）、運動する機会が減った。

一人暮らしは気楽な（　）、寂しいこともある。

Recap: Choose ～反面 when the sentence needs this relationship: on the other hand; while it is true that… .

Hold another role, action, or group in view

3. ～一方（で）

Core meaning: while; on the other hand; at the same time

Formation

V普通形／イA普通形／ナAな・である／Nである ＋ 一方（で）

Nuance and use

Two simultaneous roles or facts, or two groups that divide the situation into separate sides.

一方（で） presents another action, responsibility, feeling, trend, or group. The two sides may contrast, but they can also simply coexist: a professor researches while caring for students, or a person feels joy and responsibility at the same time.

Because the pattern is broader than 反面, it is useful when the second side is not a drawback. It can also introduce two populations: some people like talking, while others do not.

Compare

～反面 strongly suggests an opposite face, often advantage versus disadvantage. ～一方（で） can contrast, but it can also add a parallel duty or feeling.

Common mistake

Do not confuse this with the separate trend pattern ～一方だ, meaning that a change continues in one direction.

Examples

Book example

では自分のを言う一方で、ほかの人の話もよく聞いてください。

In meetings, state your own opinion while also listening carefully to what others say.

Why this works

Speaking and listening are two parallel responsibilities.

Book example

は新しいに取り組む一方で、しっかり学生のもしなければならない。

A professor must pursue new research while also taking proper care of students.

Why this works

Research and student care are simultaneous professional duties, not opposites.

Book example

子どもがうれしかった一方で、重いも感じた。

I was happy when my child was born, while at the same time I felt a heavy responsibility.

Why this works

Joy and responsibility coexist in the same event.

Book example

には人と話すことが好きな人がいる一方、それがな人も多い。

Some people in the world like talking with others, while many people are not good at it.

Why this works

The sentence divides people into two contrasting groups.

父は会社で働く一方で、週末はを手伝っている。

My father works at a company while also helping on a farm on weekends.

Why this works

The father maintains two different roles.

A：留学は楽しかった？ B：楽しかった一方で、言葉が通じず困ることもあった。

A: Was studying abroad enjoyable? B: It was enjoyable, while there were also times I struggled because I could not communicate.

Why this works

The answer keeps a positive experience and a difficulty in view.

が拡大する一方で、会社間のも激しくなっている。

The market is expanding, while competition among companies is also becoming more intense.

Why this works

Two business trends advance at the same time.

姉は家族の世話をする一方、自分の勉強も続けている。

My older sister takes care of the family while continuing her own studies.

Why this works

Neither role cancels the other; both continue together.

A：オンライン授業はどう？ B：場所を選ばない一方で、質問するが難しいです。

A: How are online classes? B: They can be taken anywhere, while finding the right moment to ask questions is difficult.

Why this works

The format has two coexisting features.

地域では高齢者が増える一方、子どもの数は減っており、両方に合うが求められている。

In the region, the elderly population is increasing while the number of children is decreasing, creating demand for policies suited to both.

Why this works

The sentence presents two demographic trends and their shared consequence.

Guided checks

彼は会社で働く（　）、夜は大学で学んでいる。

喜ぶ人がいる（　）、不安を感じる人もいる。

研究を進める（　）、後輩も指導している。

Recap: Choose ～一方（で） when the sentence needs this relationship: while; on the other hand; at the same time .

Replace a less accurate description

4. ～というより

Core meaning: rather than; more accurately; not so much A as B

Formation

N／V普通形／イA普通形／ナA（だ） ＋ というより

Nuance and use

A shift from A to a sharper B: “A—or rather, B.” The second expression is the speaker's preferred description.

Use this when the first word or description is not completely wrong, but the second is more accurate. The speaker revises a label, degree, motive, or impression: not merely a friend but a rival, not walking but practically running.

The first description may come from another person or from the speaker's own first thought. In conversation, a pause before というより often sounds like an on-the-spot correction.

Compare

～というより replaces a description. ～かわりに replaces an action or item, or balances a benefit with a cost.

Common mistake

Do not use this merely because two facts contrast. B must correct or refine how A is described.

Examples

Book example

ぼくと彼が？　いや、ぼくたちは友だちというよりいいなんだよ。

He and I are friends? No, rather than friends, we are good rivals.

Why this works

The speaker replaces the weaker label “friends” with the more accurate “rivals.”

Book example

美知子は歩くのが速い。歩くというよりという感じだ。

Michiko walks fast. It feels more like she is running than walking.

Why this works

“Running” conveys the speed more accurately than “walking.”

Book example

A「へえ、この、にかいたんですか。」B「頼まれて、というよりんだよ。」

A: Oh, did the company president ask you to paint this? B: Rather than ask, he ordered me to.

Why this works

The reply corrects the mild verb “ask” to the stronger and more accurate “order.”

今日は涼しいというより、少し。

Today is not so much cool as a little cold.

Why this works

The speaker moves to a stronger temperature description.

A：旅行は楽しかった？ B：楽しかったというより、よ。

A: Was the trip fun? B: Rather than fun, it was educational.

Why this works

The reply corrects the expected evaluation with a more fitting one.

この料理は辛いというより、。

This dish is salty rather than spicy.

Why this works

The speaker corrects which taste category applies.

今回の失敗は技術の問題というより、が原因だ。

This failure was caused by insufficient preparation rather than a technical problem.

Why this works

The sentence reclassifies the main cause.

A：彼は静かな人ですね。 B：静かというより、なんです。

A: He is a quiet person. B: Rather than quiet, he is shy around strangers.

Why this works

The reply gives a more precise reason for the observed quietness.

このかばんはデザインがいいというより、だから使っている。

I use this bag not so much because its design is good, but because it is practical.

Why this works

The speaker corrects the assumed reason for using the bag.

この作品は小説というより、作者自身のに近い。

This work is closer to the author's own record than to a novel.

Why this works

The sentence revises the work's genre classification.

Guided checks

この部屋は狭い（　）、物が多すぎる。

怒っている（　）、心配しているようだ。

休暇（　）、家で仕事をしていた。

Recap: Choose ～というより when the sentence needs this relationship: rather than; more accurately; not so much A as B .

Substitute one thing or balance a trade-off

5. ～かわりに

Core meaning: instead of; in exchange for; in return; but in compensation

Formation

V普通形／イA普通形／ナAな／Nの ＋ かわりに

Nuance and use

A replacement, a fair exchange, or a “good point, but…” balance that feels like compensation.

This pattern has three related uses. One action or person can substitute for another; two people can exchange help; or a benefit can be balanced by a cost. The surrounding clauses tell you which relationship is intended.

In trade-off uses, both sides describe the same arrangement. In exchange uses, the people and their contributions should be clear. In substitution uses, the second action happens instead of the first.

Compare

～反面 simply reveals an opposite side. ～かわりに often suggests balance, compensation, exchange, or actual replacement.

Common mistake

Do not assume every かわりに means “instead of.” Check whether the sentence describes substitution, exchange, or a trade-off.

Examples

Book example

の仕事は自由な時間が多いかわりに、お金のことがいつもだ。

Freelance work gives you a lot of free time, but in exchange money is always a worry.

Why this works

Flexible time is balanced by financial uncertainty.

Book example

の山田さんは、があるかわりに、深く考えることはしない。

Chairman Yamada is decisive, but in exchange he does not think things through deeply.

Why this works

One personal strength is balanced by a weakness.

Book example

リーさんにを教えてもらっているかわりに、リーさんの仕事をいる。

In exchange for Lee teaching me English, I help with Lee's work.

Why this works

Each person provides something in a clear exchange.

Book example

今度のはいつものようにに帰るかわりに、とをしたい。

This New Year, instead of returning to my hometown as usual, I want to travel abroad with my parents.

Why this works

The overseas trip replaces the usual hometown visit.

今日は父のかわりに私がします。

Today I will drive in place of my father.

Why this works

One person directly substitutes for another.

A：この部屋は駅に近いですね。 B：ええ。近いかわりに、が高いんです。

A: This room is near the station. B: Yes. It is close, but the rent is high in return.

Why this works

Convenient location is balanced by higher rent.

友だちに引っ越しを手伝ってもらうかわりに、晩ご飯をごちそうする。

In exchange for my friend helping me move, I will treat them to dinner.

Why this works

The meal repays the friend's help.

紙の資料を配るかわりに、をメールで送ります。

Instead of distributing paper materials, we will send an electronic version by email.

Why this works

The electronic version substitutes for printed handouts.

A：明日の当番、かわってくれる？ B：いいよ。そのかわり、来週はお願いね。

A: Can you take my duty tomorrow? B: Sure. In return, please take mine next week.

Why this works

The speakers negotiate a balanced exchange of duties.

この制度は申請が簡単なかわりに、受けられるが少ない。

This system has a simple application process, but the support available is limited.

Why this works

Administrative ease is balanced by a smaller benefit.

Guided checks

兄に料理を作ってもらう（　）、私が皿を洗う。

電車で行く（　）、今日は自転車を使った。

この店は安い（　）、駅から遠い。

Recap: Choose ～かわりに when the sentence needs this relationship: instead of; in exchange for; in return; but in compensation .

Identify the relationship between both sides

Lesson 4 review

Decide whether the sentence compares subjects, reveals a reverse side, adds a parallel fact, corrects a label, or describes substitution and exchange.

Part 1 · Choose the form

兄は外で遊ぶの（　）、弟は家で本を読む。

この仕事は面白い（　）、責任が重い。

母は働く（　）、地域の活動にも参加している。

彼は親切（　）、おせっかいだ。

私が買い物をする（　）、弟が料理する。

都会は便利なの（　）、この村は自然が豊かだ。

その発言には驚いた（　）、納得もした。

これは会議（　）、ただの報告会だった。

Part 2 · Put the sentence in order

Arrange the two-subject contrast.

  1. 村は静かだ
  2. のに対して
  3. 都会はにぎやか
  4. な

Arrange the two-sided evaluation.

  1. 不便だ
  2. 一人旅は気楽な
  3. 何でも一人なので
  4. 反面

Arrange the corrected description.

  1. 走る感じだ
  2. 彼女は速く
  3. というより
  4. 歩く

Arrange the exchanged actions.

  1. 仕事を手伝う
  2. 英語を教えてもらう
  3. 私は相手の
  4. かわりに

Part 3 · Complete the mini-passage

新しい町は便利なの（　）、古い町は落ち着いている。

この地域は静かな（　）、交通が少し不便だ。

旅行という（　）、町を調べるための仕事だった。
