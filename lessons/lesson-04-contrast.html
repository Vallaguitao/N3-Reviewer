1. ～に対して

Formation

N ＋ · V／イA／ナAな／Nな ＋ の

Book explanation

Two separate subjects with a visible difference: Osaka versus Tokyo, women versus men, or an older child versus a younger child. Use this pattern when two clearly identified subjects show different facts, habits, or qualities. The first side establishes the comparison point; the clause after に対して supplies the contrasting side. The contrast is usually objective and balanced. It works well in reports, explanations, and comparisons between places, groups, siblings, products, or statistics. In this lesson, focus on contrast; Nに対して can also mean “toward N” in other contexts.

English study note

Core meaning: in contrast to; whereas; compared with Compare ～に対して normally compares two subjects. ～反面 reveals an opposite side of the same subject. Common mistake Do not use this contrast pattern when the sentence only adds another fact about one unchanged subject. Make both sides of the comparison clear.

Book examples

Book example

きのうはではだったの、はいいだった。

Yesterday it rained heavily in Osaka, whereas Tokyo had fine weather.

Why this works

The same day's weather is contrasted between two places.

Book example

うちのはがよくにの、はまっすぐに。

In our section, the women often go out for drinks, whereas all the men go straight home.

Why this works

Two groups in the same section have different after-work habits.

Book example

がな、はのでことがだ。

In contrast to the older son, who likes playing outside, the younger son likes playing indoors.

Why this works

The brothers are separate subjects with opposite preferences.

2. ～反面

Formation

V／イA ＋ · ナAな／ナAである／Nである ＋

Book explanation

One subject with a benefit and a cost, a strength and a weakness, or two consequences pointing in different directions. This pattern describes two opposed qualities or consequences of one subject. The first side is often positive and the second negative, but the order can be reversed. Together they give a balanced evaluation. 反面 is common in explanatory and written language. It asks the listener not to judge from only one side: freedom can bring uncertainty, convenience can weaken a skill, and extra time can reduce tension.

English study note

Core meaning: on the other hand; while it is true that… Compare ～反面 keeps the same subject and turns it over to show its reverse side. ～に対して normally changes to a second subject. Common mistake Do not use 反面 for two unrelated facts. The second clause should feel like the other face of the first.

Book examples

Book example

のはことが、ストレスも。

City life has many interesting things, but on the other hand it also brings a lot of stress.

Why this works

Interest and stress are two opposing sides of the same city life.

Book example

はな、でやらなければならないので、だ。

Traveling alone is relaxing, but it is also inconvenient because you must do everything by yourself.

Why this works

Freedom and inconvenience are inseparable sides of solo travel.

Book example

をなが、もなくなってしまった。

After quitting my job, I gained more free time, but I also lost my sense of tension and purpose.

Why this works

The same decision produces one welcome and one unwelcome result.

3. ～一方（で）

Formation

V／イA／ナAな・である／Nである ＋ （で）

Book explanation

Two simultaneous roles or facts, or two groups that divide the situation into separate sides. 一方（で） presents another action, responsibility, feeling, trend, or group. The two sides may contrast, but they can also simply coexist: a professor researches while caring for students, or a person feels joy and responsibility at the same time. Because the pattern is broader than 反面, it is useful when the second side is not a drawback. It can also introduce two populations: some people like talking, while others do not.

English study note

Core meaning: while; on the other hand; at the same time Compare ～反面 strongly suggests an opposite face, often advantage versus disadvantage. ～一方（で） can contrast, but it can also add a parallel duty or feeling. Common mistake Do not confuse this with the separate trend pattern ～一方だ, meaning that a change continues in one direction.

Book examples

Book example

ではのをで、ほかののもよくください。

In meetings, state your own opinion while also listening carefully to what others say.

Why this works

Speaking and listening are two parallel responsibilities.

Book example

はにで、しっかりのもしなければならない。

A professor must pursue new research while also taking proper care of students.

Why this works

Research and student care are simultaneous professional duties, not opposites.

Book example

がうれしかったで、もじた。

I was happy when my child was born, while at the same time I felt a heavy responsibility.

Why this works

Joy and responsibility coexist in the same event.

Book example

にはとことがながいる、それがなも。

Some people in the world like talking with others, while many people are not good at it.

Why this works

The sentence divides people into two contrasting groups.

4. ～というより

Formation

N／V／イA／ナA（だ） ＋ というより

Book explanation

A shift from A to a sharper B: “A—or rather, B.” The second expression is the speaker's preferred description. Use this when the first word or description is not completely wrong, but the second is more accurate. The speaker revises a label, degree, motive, or impression: not merely a friend but a rival, not walking but practically running. The first description may come from another person or from the speaker's own first thought. In conversation, a pause before というより often sounds like an on-the-spot correction.

English study note

Core meaning: rather than; more accurately; not so much A as B Compare ～というより replaces a description. ～かわりに replaces an action or item, or balances a benefit with a cost. Common mistake Do not use this merely because two facts contrast. B must correct or refine how A is described.

Book examples

Book example

ぼくとが？　いや、ぼくたちはというよりいいなんだよ。

He and I are friends? No, rather than friends, we are good rivals.

Why this works

The speaker replaces the weaker label “friends” with the more accurate “rivals.”

Book example

はのが。というよりというじだ。

Michiko walks fast. It feels more like she is running than walking.

Why this works

“Running” conveys the speed more accurately than “walking.”

Book example

A「へえ、この、にれてかいたんですか。」B「れて、というよりされたんだよ。」

A: Oh, did the company president ask you to paint this? B: Rather than ask, he ordered me to.

Why this works

The reply corrects the mild verb “ask” to the stronger and more accurate “order.”

5. ～かわりに

Formation

V／イA／ナAな／Nの ＋ かわりに

Book explanation

A replacement, a fair exchange, or a “good point, but…” balance that feels like compensation. This pattern has three related uses. One action or person can substitute for another; two people can exchange help; or a benefit can be balanced by a cost. The surrounding clauses tell you which relationship is intended. In trade-off uses, both sides describe the same arrangement. In exchange uses, the people and their contributions should be clear. In substitution uses, the second action happens instead of the first.

English study note

Core meaning: instead of; in exchange for; in return; but in compensation Compare ～反面 simply reveals an opposite side. ～かわりに often suggests balance, compensation, exchange, or actual replacement. Common mistake Do not assume every かわりに means “instead of.” Check whether the sentence describes substitution, exchange, or a trade-off.

Book examples

Book example

フリーのはながかわりに、のことがいつもだ。

Freelance work gives you a lot of free time, but in exchange money is always a worry.

Why this works

Flexible time is balanced by financial uncertainty.

Book example

のは、があるかわりに、ことはしない。

Chairman Yamada is decisive, but in exchange he does not think things through deeply.

Why this works

One personal strength is balanced by a weakness.

Book example

リーさんにをもらっているかわりに、リーさんのをいる。

In exchange for Lee teaching me English, I help with Lee's work.

Why this works

Each person provides something in a clear exchange.

Book example

のはいつものようにふるさとにかわりに、とをしたい。

This New Year, instead of returning to my hometown as usual, I want to travel abroad with my parents.

Why this works

The overseas trip replaces the usual hometown visit.
