Match a model or expectation

～とおり／～どおり

just as; in the same way as; according to

Formation

Vる／Vた ＋ とおり（に／だ） · Nの ＋ とおり（に／だ） · N ＋ どおり（に／だ）

Use this family when an action, result, or situation matches a model, instruction, plan, or expectation. Add に when the phrase modifies an action; use だ／です when it completes the sentence.

With a noun, Nのとおり is the productive pattern. Some familiar nouns form a direct compound with どおり, such as 予定どおり、予想どおり、計画どおり.

What to listen for

A reference point first, followed by something that copies it or turns out exactly the same.

Choosing とおり or どおり

Use とおり after verbs and after Nの. Use どおり only in established noun compounds; do not attach it freely to every noun.

Common mistake

Do not say 見たどおり after a verb. The correct form is 見たとおり. The sound change belongs to direct noun compounds.

Foundation examples

でとおりにいったので、に。

Because I followed the directions I received at the police box, I reached the venue without getting lost.

Why this works

The directions from the police box are the model that the speaker followed exactly; the past verb 教えてもらった attaches directly to とおりに.

作るだから、このにあるとおりのでみよう。

Since this is my first time making the dish, I will try using the method written in this book.

Why this works

書いてあるとおりの modifies やり方: the method matches what is written in the book. The の after とおり lets the whole phrase describe a noun.

サッカーののはわたしたちのどおりだった。

The result of the soccer match was just as we had expected.

Why this works

期待どおり is an established direct noun compound meaning that the actual result matched the expectation.

Everyday situations

にとおりに、を。

I cooked the dish just as my mother taught me.

Why this works

The past verb describes the earlier instruction being followed.

のとおりに、に。

When I walked according to the map, I arrived at the station.

Why this works

The map supplies the route used as a model.

どおり、からが。

Just as the weather forecast predicted, it snowed in the afternoon.

Why this works

The actual weather matched the forecast.

Mini-dialogues

A：どのからか。
B：にとおりにください。

A: Which button should I press first?
B: Please press them in the order shown on the screen.

Why this works

The visible screen sequence is the model for the action.

A：はどうだった？
B：がとおり、よ。

A: How was the exam?
B: It was difficult, just as the teacher said.

Why this works

The result matches the teacher’s earlier statement.

Longer exam-style contexts

をにとおりに、。

The experiment succeeded as a result of following the procedure manual exactly.

Why this works

The passive instruction clause provides the procedure that was copied.

はいつもがとおりにとは。

Things do not always proceed exactly as one expects.

Why this works

The phrase establishes an expected course, which the negative expression says is not guaranteed.

Guided checks

先生が説明した（　）に、やってください。

計画（　）、工事は終わった。

地図（　）歩いた。

Identify the deciding relationship

～によって／～によっては

by means of; by; due to; depending on

Formation

N ＋ によって／により／によるN · N ＋ によっては

This formal connector has several meanings. Let the surrounding nouns and verb decide: a method produces a result, an agent performs a passive action, a cause produces an event, or a category explains variation.

～によっては narrows the category and introduces an exceptional case: “depending on X, some cases are different.” ～による is the form used directly before a noun.

Meaning map

method: by means of · passive agent: by · cause: due to · variation: differs depending on · exception: in some cases, depending on

Compare with で

For an ordinary physical tool, で is usually more natural: ペンで書く. によって sounds formal and suits systems, methods, causes, creators, and category-based differences.

Common mistake

Do not translate every English “by” as によって. Check whether the sentence is passive, formal, or describing a systematic relationship.

Foundation examples

によってが。

Customs differ depending on the country.

Why this works

国によって marks country as the category that determines variation in customs.

はによってだ。

The way people feel about things varies from person to person.

Why this works

人によって shows that the result varies according to the individual.

わたしのは。日によってはになることもある。

The time I get home differs every day. On some days, it can be the middle of the night.

Why this works

日によって first gives the varying category; 日によっては then narrows it to exceptional days that end very late.

Everyday situations

によってはのはになるかもしれない。

Depending on the circumstances, this year’s cultural festival may be cancelled.

Why this works

場合によっては introduces a possible exceptional outcome depending on the circumstances.

によっては、がなこともある。

Depending on the person, some may dislike spicy food.

Why this works

は highlights an exceptional subgroup among people.

によって、が。

The scenery changes greatly depending on the season.

Why this works

Season is the variable that controls the change.

Mini-dialogues

A：このはどうやっての？
B：のによってよ。

A: How did you collect this data?
B: I collected it through an online survey.

Why this works

The survey is a systematic method of collection.

A：どのもですか。
B：いいえ、によってはのを。

A: Does every class use the same textbook?
B: No. Some classes use a different one.

Why this works

によっては introduces exceptions within the class category.

Longer exam-style contexts

このなはによってが、のによって。

This historic temple was destroyed by fire but rebuilt by local people.

Why this works

The first によって marks cause; the second marks the agents of a passive action.

のはによってため、をがある。

Because survey results vary depending on the response method, the method must be standardized.

Why this works

The response method is the category across which results vary.

Guided checks

この小説は有名な作家（　）書かれた。

交通事故（　）、バスが遅れている。

国（　）、習慣が違う。

The same trigger, a recurring result

～たびに

every time; whenever; on each occasion

Formation

Vる ＋ たびに · Nの ＋ たびに

Use this pattern when the same kind of event occurs repeatedly and each occurrence triggers another action, feeling, discovery, or change. The listener should be able to imagine more than one real occasion.

The result does not have to be intentional, but it normally recurs in a recognizable way. This makes ～たびに useful for habits, memories, repeated problems, and gradual change noticed from one occasion to the next.

What to listen for

A repeatable trigger followed by “again, on that occasion” rather than one continuous period.

Compare with とき and ごとに

～とき can describe one occasion and does not require repetition. ～ごとに is useful for fixed units or every member of a set, such as every three days; ～たびに focuses on each actual occurrence.

Common mistake

Do not use ～たびに for a single past event or a condition that simply continues without separate occasions.

Foundation examples

このはが来るたびにのが。

Every time a typhoon comes to this region, damaging floods occur.

Why this works

Each typhoon is a repeated trigger, and flooding occurs again on every occurrence.

はわたしがをかけるたびに、ちゃんとをいるかと。

Every time I call, my mother asks whether I am eating proper meals.

Why this works

Every phone call repeatedly triggers the same question from the speaker’s mother.

このチームはのたびになっていく。

This team gets stronger with every match.

Why this works

The noun 試合 takes のたびに, and each match brings another change toward becoming stronger.

Everyday situations

そのをたびに、を。

Whenever I hear that song, I remember my student days.

Why this works

Repeated hearings call up the same period of life.

そのにたびに、を。

Every time I go to that restaurant, I try a new menu item.

Why this works

Each visit creates a new occasion for the habit.

そのはたびに、がなっている。

Each time I see that child, they have grown taller.

Why this works

Separate meetings reveal a repeated gradual change.

Mini-dialogues

A：このによくの？
B：うん。たびにをよ。

A: Do you come to this café often?
B: Yes. I order a different cake every time.

Why this works

Each visit triggers a fresh choice.

A：にする？
B：うん。するたびに、かどうかよ。

A: Do you call your grandmother?
B: Yes. Every time I call, she asks whether I am well.

Why this works

The same question recurs on each phone call.

Longer exam-style contexts

をするたびに、が。

Each time we conduct an evacuation drill, a new problem is discovered.

Why this works

Each drill is a separate occasion that yields another finding.

がたびに、をなければならない。

Every time the software is updated, I have to learn a new way to operate it.

Why this works

Each update repeatedly creates the same learning burden.

Guided checks

この曲を聞く（　）、高校時代を思い出す。

旅行（　）、たくさん写真を撮る。

先週京都へ行った（　）、古い寺を見た。

Track degree and proportional change

～（ば）～ほど／～ほど

the more…the more; to the extent that; as…as

Formation

Vば Vるほど · いAければ いAいほど · なAなら なAなほど · いAい／なAな ＋ N ＋ ほど · clause ＋ ほど

In the paired pattern, the first condition increases and the second result changes in proportion: “the more A, the more B.” The repeated word makes the relationship explicit.

Single ほど marks a degree or limit, as in “so tired that I could not stand.” Context tells you whether you are reading a proportional pair, a high degree, or a comparison such as Nほど～ない.

Form map

勉強すればするほど · 少なければ少ないほど · 静かなら静かなほど · 経験が多い人ほど

Paired versus single ほど

練習すればするほど上手になる links two changing scales. 立てないほど疲れた uses one result to show how extreme the tiredness was.

Common mistake

The two scales must have a logical relationship. With a noun phrase, put ほど after the full phrase, as in 経験が多い人ほど; do not force a category noun into an artificial scale.

Foundation examples

が増えるほどがになる。

The more things you have, the harder they become to organize.

Why this works

The repeated conditional pattern 増えれば増えるほど links a greater quantity with a proportionally greater organizing burden.

本当にいいはがたつほどが。

Truly good furniture increases in value as time passes.

Why this works

時間がたつほど shows a continuing proportional relationship: more elapsed time brings higher value.

の日は多いほど。

The more days off I have, the happier I am.

Why this works

多ければ多いほど repeats the i-adjective and means the greater the number of days off, the greater the happiness.

Everyday situations

がなほどではがよくのだ。

The livelier a town is, the better goods sell in its shops.

Why this works

A na-adjective takes な before ほど. Greater liveliness corresponds to stronger sales.

人ほどのがだ。

The busier a person is, the better they tend to be at using time.

Why this works

Nほど highlights the group at the stronger end of a scale: especially busy people are skilled at using time.

はほどいた。

Yesterday I was so tired that I could not stand.

Why this works

This is single ほど: the impossible result shows the degree of tiredness.

Mini-dialogues

A：どのがいい？
B：ほどよ。

A: Which suitcase is best?
B: The lighter it is, the better for me.

Why this works

Decreasing weight increases convenience.

A：どうしたらになる？
B：するほどになるよ。

A: How can I improve?
B: The more you practice, the better you become.

Why this works

More practice produces increasing skill.

Longer exam-style contexts

はほどよいとは。なものをもだ。

More information is not always better. The ability to choose what is necessary also matters.

Why this works

The proportional frame is deliberately limited by とは限らない.

はほど、のがなる。

The higher a mountain is, the lower the temperature at its summit becomes.

Why this works

The two degree scales move in opposite directions.

Guided checks

日本語は話せば（　）ほど、楽しくなる。

部屋は静かなら静か（　）、勉強しやすい。

声が出ない（　）驚いた。

Use one trip for a useful extra

～ついでに

while you are at it; taking the opportunity to

Formation

Vる／Vた ＋ ついでに · Nの ＋ ついでに

Use this pattern when a main action creates a convenient chance to do a secondary action. The clause before ついでに gives the original purpose or larger task; the clause after it gives the useful extra.

The actions are normally carried out by the same person and fit the same route, place, or occasion. They may be sequential rather than exactly simultaneous.

Ask one question

If the extra action were removed, which action would still be the reason for the trip or task? Put that main action before ついでに.

Compare with ながら and simple ～て

～ながら emphasizes two actions happening at the same time. Simple ～て reports sequence without ranking the actions. ～ついでに explicitly treats the second action as a convenient extra.

Common mistake

Do not reverse the actions if the supposed “extra” is actually the main purpose. The speaker’s real reason for going or doing should come first.

Foundation examples

のついでにこのをにきて。

While you are out for a walk, please mail this postcard.

Why this works

The walk is the main outing; mailing the postcard is the convenient extra action.

のをするついでにのをしよう。

While cleaning the entrance, let’s organize the shoes too.

Why this works

Cleaning the entrance is the main task, and organizing shoes uses the same place and opportunity.

でをついでにDVDも。

When I ordered a book online, I also looked up newly released DVDs.

Why this works

Ordering the book is the original online task; checking new DVDs is added while already using the site.

Everyday situations

をついでに、を。

While cleaning the room, I also threw away old books.

Why this works

Sorting and discarding become a convenient extra within the cleaning task.

をついでに、ももらった。

When I had my computer repaired, I also had its memory upgraded.

Why this works

The repair visit creates the chance to request a secondary upgrade.

までをについでに、を。

While going to the station to meet a friend, I picked up a ticket I had reserved.

Why this works

The station trip is already necessary, so ticket pickup is an efficient extra.

Mini-dialogues

A：にの？
B：うん。ついでに、もか。

A: Are you going to the convenience store?
B: Yes. Shall I buy drinks while I am there?

Why this works

The drink purchase is offered as an extra to an existing trip.

A：へするの？
B：うん。のついでに、のにもつもり。

A: Are you going to Tokyo on business?
B: Yes. I plan to meet a university friend while I am there.

Why this works

The business trip is primary; the reunion uses the same opportunity.

Longer exam-style contexts

でのをするついでに、なも。

While completing an address-change procedure at city hall, I also applied for a necessary certificate.

Why this works

Both tasks use the same counter, but the address change is the original purpose.

でをついでに、を。

During the regular equipment inspection, we also replaced old parts.

Why this works

The inspection provides a practical opportunity for the secondary maintenance task.

Guided checks

勉強しに図書館へ行く（　）、本を返してきます。

出張（　）、友達に会った。

音楽を聞き（　）、勉強する。

Identify the relationship before choosing a form: matching, cause or variation, recurring trigger, changing degree, or a convenient extra.

Part 1 · Choose the form

Hint

A verb clause must come directly before とおり.

人生は自分の（　）とおりにはいかない。

Hint

The painting is the source showing what the area used to be like.

この絵の（　）30年前はこの辺は畑だった。

Hint

によって introduces a category across which something varies.

この虫は地方によって呼び方が（　）そうだ。

Hint

によっては singles out some places as possible exceptions.

あしたは、所によっては（　）。

Hint

たびに needs an action that happens repeatedly on every date.

彼女はデートのたびに（　）。

Hint

Complete the repeated conditional pattern Vば Vるほど.

この絵は本物ではないが、見れば（　）本物に見える。

Hint

A na-adjective uses なら in the conditional half and な before ほど.

刺身は（　）新鮮なほどおいしい。

Hint

The piano playing and singing happen simultaneously.

カンさんはピアノを（　）歌を歌うのが上手だ。

Hint

The second action should be a convenient extra on the same trip.

銀行に行ったついでに（　）。
