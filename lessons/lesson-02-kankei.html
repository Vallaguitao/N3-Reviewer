1. ～とおり／～どおり

Formation

Vる／Vた ＋ とおり（に／だ） · Nの ＋ とおり（に／だ） · N ＋ どおり（に／だ）

Book explanation

Use this family when an action, result, or situation matches a model, instruction, plan, or expectation. Add に when the phrase modifies an action; use だ／です when it completes the sentence. With a noun, Nのとおり is the productive pattern. Some familiar nouns form a direct compound with どおり, such as 予定どおり、予想どおり、計画どおり. A reference point first, followed by something that copies it or turns out exactly the same.

English study note

Core meaning: just as; in the same way as; according to Compare Use とおり after verbs and after Nの. Use どおり only in established noun compounds; do not attach it freely to every noun. Common mistake Do not say 見たどおり after a verb. The correct form is 見たとおり. The sound change belongs to direct noun compounds.

Book examples

Book example

でえてもらったとおりにいていったので、わずに。

Because I followed the directions I received at the police box, I reached the venue without getting lost.

Why this works

The directions from the police box are the model that the speaker followed exactly; the past verb 教えてもらった attaches directly to とおりに.

Book example

だから、このにいてあるとおりのでってみよう。

Since this is my first time making the dish, I will try using the method written in this book.

Why this works

書いてあるとおりの modifies やり方: the method matches what is written in the book. The の after とおり lets the whole phrase describe a noun.

Book example

サッカーののはわたしたちのどおりだった。

The result of the soccer match was just as we had expected.

Why this works

期待どおり is an established direct noun compound meaning that the actual result matched the expectation.

2. ～によって／～によっては

Formation

N ＋ によって／により／によるN · N ＋ によっては

Book explanation

This formal connector has several meanings. Let the surrounding nouns and verb decide: a method produces a result, an agent performs a passive action, a cause produces an event, or a category explains variation. ～によっては narrows the category and introduces an exceptional case: “depending on X, some cases are different.” ～による is the form used directly before a noun. method: by means of · passive agent: by · cause: due to · variation: differs depending on · exception: in some cases, depending on

English study note

Core meaning: by means of; by; due to; depending on Compare For an ordinary physical tool, で is usually more natural: ペンで書く. によって sounds formal and suits systems, methods, causes, creators, and category-based differences. Common mistake Do not translate every English “by” as によって. Check whether the sentence is passive, formal, or describing a systematic relationship.

Book examples

Book example

によってが。

Customs differ depending on the country.

Why this works

国によって marks country as the category that determines variation in customs.

Book example

はによってだ。

The way people feel about things varies from person to person.

Why this works

人によって shows that the result varies according to the individual.

Book example

わたしのは。によってはになることもある。

The time I get home differs every day. On some days, it can be the middle of the night.

Why this works

日によって first gives the varying category; 日によっては then narrows it to exceptional days that end very late.

Book example

によってはのはになるかもしれない。

Depending on the circumstances, this year’s cultural festival may be cancelled.

Why this works

場合によっては introduces a possible exceptional outcome depending on the circumstances.

3. ～たびに

Formation

Vる ＋ たびに · Nの ＋ たびに

Book explanation

Use this pattern when the same kind of event occurs repeatedly and each occurrence triggers another action, feeling, discovery, or change. The listener should be able to imagine more than one real occasion. The result does not have to be intentional, but it normally recurs in a recognizable way. This makes ～たびに useful for habits, memories, repeated problems, and gradual change noticed from one occasion to the next. A repeatable trigger followed by “again, on that occasion” rather than one continuous period.

English study note

Core meaning: every time; whenever; on each occasion Compare ～とき can describe one occasion and does not require repetition. ～ごとに is useful for fixed units or every member of a set, such as every three days; ～たびに focuses on each actual occurrence. Common mistake Do not use ～たびに for a single past event or a condition that simply continues without separate occasions.

Book examples

Book example

このはがたびにのが。

Every time a typhoon comes to this region, damaging floods occur.

Why this works

Each typhoon is a repeated trigger, and flooding occurs again on every occurrence.

Book example

はわたしがをかけるたびに、ちゃんとをべてかと。

Every time I call, my mother asks whether I am eating proper meals.

Why this works

Every phone call repeatedly triggers the same question from the speaker’s mother.

Book example

このチームはのたびになっていく。

This team gets stronger with every match.

Why this works

The noun 試合 takes のたびに, and each match brings another change toward becoming stronger.

4. ～（ば）～ほど／～ほど

Formation

Vば Vるほど · いAければ いAいほど · なAなら なAなほど · いAい／なAな ＋ N ＋ ほど · clause ＋ ほど

Book explanation

In the paired pattern, the first condition increases and the second result changes in proportion: “the more A, the more B.” The repeated word makes the relationship explicit. Single ほど marks a degree or limit, as in “so tired that I could not stand.” Context tells you whether you are reading a proportional pair, a high degree, or a comparison such as Nほど～ない. 勉強すればするほど · 少なければ少ないほど · 静かなら静かなほど · 経験が多い人ほど

English study note

Core meaning: the more…the more; to the extent that; as…as Compare 練習すればするほど上手になる links two changing scales. 立てないほど疲れた uses one result to show how extreme the tiredness was. Common mistake The two scales must have a logical relationship. With a noun phrase, put ほど after the full phrase, as in 経験が多い人ほど; do not force a category noun into an artificial scale.

Book examples

Book example

がえればほどがになる。

The more things you have, the harder they become to organize.

Why this works

The repeated conditional pattern 増えれば増えるほど links a greater quantity with a proportionally greater organizing burden.

Book example

にいいはがたつほどが。

Truly good furniture increases in value as time passes.

Why this works

時間がたつほど shows a continuing proportional relationship: more elapsed time brings higher value.

Book example

のはほどうれしい。

The more days off I have, the happier I am.

Why this works

多ければ多いほど repeats the i-adjective and means the greater the number of days off, the greater the happiness.

Book example

がなほどではがよくのだ。

The livelier a town is, the better goods sell in its shops.

Why this works

A na-adjective takes な before ほど. Greater liveliness corresponds to stronger sales.

Book example

ほどのがだ。

The busier a person is, the better they tend to be at using time.

Why this works

Nほど highlights the group at the stronger end of a scale: especially busy people are skilled at using time.

5. ～ついでに

Formation

Vる／Vた ＋ ついでに · Nの ＋ ついでに

Book explanation

Use this pattern when a main action creates a convenient chance to do a secondary action. The clause before ついでに gives the original purpose or larger task; the clause after it gives the useful extra. The actions are normally carried out by the same person and fit the same route, place, or occasion. They may be sequential rather than exactly simultaneous. If the extra action were removed, which action would still be the reason for the trip or task? Put that main action before ついでに.

English study note

Core meaning: while you are at it; taking the opportunity to Compare ～ながら emphasizes two actions happening at the same time. Simple ～て reports sequence without ranking the actions. ～ついでに explicitly treats the second action as a convenient extra. Common mistake Do not reverse the actions if the supposed “extra” is actually the main purpose. The speaker’s real reason for going or doing should come first.

Book examples

Book example

のついでにこのをにしてきて。

While you are out for a walk, please mail this postcard.

Why this works

The walk is the main outing; mailing the postcard is the convenient extra action.

Book example

のをするついでにのをしよう。

While cleaning the entrance, let’s organize the shoes too.

Why this works

Cleaning the entrance is the main task, and organizing shoes uses the same place and opportunity.

Book example

でをついでにしくたDVDもべた。

When I ordered a book online, I also looked up newly released DVDs.

Why this works

Ordering the book is the original online task; checking new DVDs is added while already using the site.
