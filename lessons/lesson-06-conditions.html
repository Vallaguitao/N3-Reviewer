Respond to information or a stated situation

1. ～（の）なら…

Core meaning: Use ～なら when your condition is based on information, appearance, or a situation introduced in the conversation.

Formation

普通形 + （の）なら／ナA・N + なら

Nuance and use

The main clause commonly gives advice, a judgment, an offer, or the speaker's intention in response to the condition.

Compare

Use ～たら for an event that happens first and ～ば for a logical condition. ～なら often reacts to what someone has just said.

Common mistake

Nouns and na-adjectives normally attach directly to なら, not のなら.

Examples

Book example

その、もう使わないんですか。使わないならわたしにください。

Are you no longer using that box? If not, please give it to me.

Why this works

The request responds to the information that the box is no longer needed.

Book example

ああ、あしたはか。ならサイクリングには行けそうもないね。

Oh, it will rain tomorrow? If it rains, it looks as though we cannot go cycling.

Why this works

The judgment is based on newly received weather information.

Book example

その本、読んでしまったのならわたしに貸してくれませんか。

If you have finished reading that book, could you lend it to me?

Why this works

のなら takes the completed action as the basis for a request.

パソコンを買うなら、軽いものがいいですよ。

If you are buying a computer, a light one is a good choice.

Why this works

This example applies ～（の）なら… in a natural situation.

京都へ行くなら、秋がおすすめです。

If you are going to Kyoto, autumn is recommended.

Why this works

This example applies ～（の）なら… in a natural situation.

手伝いがなら、いつでも言ってください。

If you need help, tell me anytime.

Why this works

This example applies ～（の）なら… in a natural situation.

参加するのなら、今日中に申し込んでください。

If you intend to participate, apply by today.

Why this works

This example applies ～（の）なら… in a natural situation.

辛い物が苦手なら、この料理は避けたほうがいい。

If you dislike spicy food, you should avoid this dish.

Why this works

This example applies ～（の）なら… in a natural situation.

駅へ行くなら、この手紙を出してくれませんか。

If you are going to the station, could you mail this letter?

Why this works

This example applies ～（の）なら… in a natural situation.

Why this works

This example applies ～（の）なら… in a natural situation.

Guided checks

京都へ行く（　）、秋がおすすめです。

手伝いが必要（　）、言ってください。

参加する（　）、今日申し込んでください。

Recap: Use ～なら when your condition is based on information, appearance, or a situation introduced in the conversation.

Predict an undesirable result

2. ～ては…・～（の）では…

Core meaning: Use ～ては or ～のでは when the stated fact or assumed situation leads to an unfavorable result.

Formation

Vて／イAくて／ナAで／Nで + は／普通形 + のでは

Nuance and use

The following clause is normally a negative evaluation, difficulty, or unwanted outcome rather than a hope, invitation, or request.

Compare

～なら can support advice or intention. ～ては／～のでは focuses on the bad consequence of the condition itself.

Common mistake

Do not follow this pattern with the speaker's simple hope, invitation, or command.

Examples

Book example

山中さんはしたばかりだから、おに行ってはかえってだろう。

Mr. Yamanaka has just had surgery, so visiting him would probably be more of a burden.

Why this works

Going to visit is evaluated as producing an undesirable result.

Book example

そんななではみんなにきらわれますよ。

With such an irresponsible attitude, everyone will dislike you.

Why this works

The attitude leads to a negative social consequence.

Book example

今から家を建て始めるのではにはでき上がらない。

If construction starts only now, the house will not be finished within the year.

Why this works

The assumed late start makes completion impossible.

こんなにが降っては、試合はできない。

If it rains this heavily, the match cannot be held.

Why this works

This example shows ～ては…・～（の）では… in a distinct everyday context.

準備が不十分では、発表は成功しないだろう。

With insufficient preparation, the presentation will probably not succeed.

Why this works

This example shows ～ては…・～（の）では… in a distinct everyday context.

毎日遅刻していては、信用を失いますよ。

If you keep arriving late every day, you will lose people's trust.

Why this works

This example shows ～ては…・～（の）では… in a distinct everyday context.

説明を読まないのでは、正しく使えない。

If you do not read the instructions, you cannot use it correctly.

Why this works

This example shows ～ては…・～（の）では… in a distinct everyday context.

部屋が暗くては、本が読めない。

If the room is dark, I cannot read.

Why this works

This example shows ～ては…・～（の）では… in a distinct everyday context.

そんなに高いのでは、学生には買えない。

If it is that expensive, students cannot buy it.

Why this works

This example shows ～ては…・～（の）では… in a distinct everyday context.

このまま何もしないのでは、問題は解決しない。

If nothing is done, the problem will not be solved.

Why this works

This example shows ～ては…・～（の）では… in a distinct everyday context.

Guided checks

雨がこんなに降っ（　）、試合はできない。

準備が不十分で（　）、成功しない。

今から始めるの（　）、間に合わない。

Recap: Use ～ては or ～のでは when the stated fact or assumed situation leads to an unfavorable result.

Name the one sufficient minimum

3. ～さえ～ば…・～さえ～なら…

Core meaning: Use ～さえ～ば or ～さえ～なら to say that one condition alone is enough for the result.

Formation

N + さえ + Vば／イAければ／ナA・Nなら

Nuance and use

さえ highlights the minimum requirement. Everything else is treated as less important once that one condition is met.

Compare

だけ means only, but does not itself create a sufficient condition. さえ～ば explicitly means 'if only this condition is met.'

Common mistake

The predicate following さえ must take an appropriate conditional form such as ～ば or ～なら.

Examples

Book example

太郎はさえ読んでいればしないようだ。

Taro seems not to get bored as long as he can read manga.

Why this works

Manga is presented as the one sufficient minimum for avoiding boredom.

Book example

体さえならどんなことにもできる。

As long as one is physically healthy, one can take on any challenge.

Why this works

Physical health alone is the sufficient condition.

Book example

一言「ごめんなさい。」と言いさえすれば、は許してくれるだろう。

If you only say one word, 'I'm sorry,' the other person will probably forgive you.

Why this works

言いさえすれば highlights the one action sufficient for forgiveness.

住所さえわかれば、手紙を送れます。

If only I know the address, I can send the letter.

Why this works

This example shows ～さえ～ば…・～さえ～なら… in a distinct everyday context.

時間さえあれば、この本を読み終えられる。

If only I have time, I can finish this book.

Why this works

This example shows ～さえ～ば…・～さえ～なら… in a distinct everyday context.

静かでさえあれば、どの部屋でもいい。

Any room is fine as long as it is quiet.

Why this works

This example shows ～さえ～ば…・～さえ～なら… in a distinct everyday context.

名前さえ書けば、申し込みは完了です。

The application is complete if you only write your name.

Why this works

This example shows ～さえ～ば…・～さえ～なら… in a distinct everyday context.

水さえあれば、しばらく生活できる。

As long as there is water, we can manage for a while.

Why this works

This example shows ～さえ～ば…・～さえ～なら… in a distinct everyday context.

に気持ちを伝えさえすれば、誤解は解けるだろう。

If you just convey your feelings, the misunderstanding will probably clear up.

Why this works

This example shows ～さえ～ば…・～さえ～なら… in a distinct everyday context.

健康でさえあれば、新しいことにできる。

As long as you are healthy, you can take on something new.

Why this works

This example shows ～さえ～ば…・～さえ～なら… in a distinct everyday context.

Guided checks

住所（　）わかれば、送れます。

水（　）あれば、生活できる。

Recap: Use ～さえ～ば or ～さえ～なら to say that one condition alone is enough for the result.

Keep the result despite an extreme condition

4. たとえ～ても…・たとえ～でも…

Core meaning: Use たとえ～ても or たとえ～でも to say the main result, decision, or judgment remains true even under an extreme condition.

Formation

たとえ + Vても／イAくても／ナA・Nでも

Nuance and use

たとえ adds emphasis and imagines a difficult, unlikely, or extreme case. The following clause refuses to change.

Compare

ても alone means even if. Adding たとえ makes the concession stronger and more deliberate.

Common mistake

たとえ normally pairs with a concessive ending such as ～ても or ～でも, not a plain conditional.

Examples

Book example

たとえの人たちにどんなにされても、ぼくはプロのになりたい。

Even if everyone around me opposes it, I want to become a professional singer.

Why this works

たとえ pairs with ても to keep the goal unchanged despite opposition.

Book example

たとえ高くても、仕事になものは買わなければならない。

Even if it is expensive, we must buy what is necessary for the job.

Why this works

The obligation remains even under the extreme condition of high cost.

Book example

たとえでも、は毎年受けたほうがいいですよ。

Even if it is troublesome, you should have a health examination every year.

Why this works

The recommendation remains despite the inconvenience.

たとえ失敗しても、もう一度する。

Even if I fail, I will try again.

Why this works

This example shows たとえ～ても…・たとえ～でも… in a distinct everyday context.

たとえでも、予定どおり出発します。

Even if it rains, we will depart as scheduled.

Why this works

This example shows たとえ～ても…・たとえ～でも… in a distinct everyday context.

たとえが許さなくても、きちんと謝りたい。

Even if the other person does not forgive me, I want to apologize properly.

Why this works

This example shows たとえ～ても…・たとえ～でも… in a distinct everyday context.

たとえ遠くても、その店へ行く価値がある。

Even if it is far away, that shop is worth visiting.

Why this works

This example shows たとえ～ても…・たとえ～でも… in a distinct everyday context.

たとえにされても、自分の考えを説明する。

Even if people around me oppose it, I will explain my view.

Why this works

This example shows たとえ～ても…・たとえ～でも… in a distinct everyday context.

たとえでも、な手続きをしなければならない。

Even if it is troublesome, the required procedure must be completed.

Why this works

This example shows たとえ～ても…・たとえ～でも… in a distinct everyday context.

たとえになれなくても、音楽は続けたい。

Even if I cannot become a singer, I want to continue with music.

Why this works

This example shows たとえ～ても…・たとえ～でも… in a distinct everyday context.

Guided checks

（　）失敗しても、挑戦する。

（　）雨でも、出発する。

（　）反対されても、続ける。

Recap: Use たとえ～ても or たとえ～でも to say the main result, decision, or judgment remains true even under an extreme condition.

Imagine a contrary condition with regret

5. ～ば…のに／～たら…のに／～なら…のに

Core meaning: Use a conditional plus のに to imagine a result that differs from reality and express regret, disappointment, or an unreal wish.

Formation

Vば／Vたら／N・ナAなら + result + のに

Nuance and use

The conditional identifies what would have made the desired result possible. のに signals that reality did not match that imagined outcome.

Compare

Without のに, these forms can state ordinary conditions. With のに, the sentence looks back or contrasts with reality.

Common mistake

Keep the time relationship clear: ～たら often imagines a completed past condition; ～なら can imagine an identity or situation.

Examples

Book example

お金とひまがあればわたしもするんだけど……。

If I had money and free time, I would travel overseas too.

Why this works

あれば describes an unreal present condition and んだけど leaves the wish unfinished.

Book example

もししていたらこのには乗れなかった。間に合ってよかった。

If I had overslept, I could not have boarded this plane. I am glad I made it.

Why this works

ていたら imagines a past condition contrary to what actually happened.

Book example

ああ、だ。学生ならでチケットが買えたのに……。

What a shame. If I were a student, I could have bought the ticket with a student discount.

Why this works

なら presents the unreal identity condition behind the regret.

もっと早く出れば、電車に間に合ったのに。

If I had left earlier, I would have caught the train.

Why this works

This example shows ～ば…のに／～たら…のに／～なら…のに in a distinct everyday context.

地図を見ていたら、道に迷わなかったのに。

If I had checked the map, I would not have gotten lost.

Why this works

This example shows ～ば…のに／～たら…のに／～なら…のに in a distinct everyday context.

学生なら、このが使えたのに。

If I were a student, I could have used this student discount.

Why this works

This example shows ～ば…のに／～たら…のに／～なら…のに in a distinct everyday context.

もう少し安ければ、買えるのに。

If it were a little cheaper, I could buy it.

Why this works

This example shows ～ば…のに／～たら…のに／～なら…のに in a distinct everyday context.

昨日申し込んでいたら、間に合ったのに。

If I had applied yesterday, I would have made the deadline.

Why this works

This example shows ～ば…のに／～たら…のに／～なら…のに in a distinct everyday context.

健康なら、にも行けるのに。

If I were healthy, I could travel overseas too.

Why this works

This example shows ～ば…のに／～たら…のに／～なら…のに in a distinct everyday context.

しなかったら、に間に合ったのに。

If I had not overslept, I would have caught the plane.

Why this works

This example shows ～ば…のに／～たら…のに／～なら…のに in a distinct everyday context.

Guided checks

もっと勉強してい（　）、合格できたのに。

早く出てい（　）、間に合ったのに。

寝坊しなかっ（　）、飛行機に乗れたのに。

Recap: Use a conditional plus のに to imagine a result that differs from reality and express regret, disappointment, or an unreal wish.
