1. ～（の）なら…

Formation

普通形 + （の）なら／ナA・N + なら

Book explanation

The main clause commonly gives advice, a judgment, an offer, or the speaker's intention in response to the condition.

English study note

Core meaning: Use ～なら when your condition is based on information, appearance, or a situation introduced in the conversation. CompareUse ～たら for an event that happens first and ～ば for a logical condition. ～なら often reacts to what someone has just said. Common mistakeNouns and na-adjectives normally attach directly to なら, not のなら.

Book examples

Book example

その、もうんですか。ならわたしにください。

Are you no longer using that box? If not, please give it to me.

Why this works

The request responds to the information that the box is no longer needed.

Book example

ああ、あしたはか。ならサイクリングにはけそうもないね。

Oh, it will rain tomorrow? If it rains, it looks as though we cannot go cycling.

Why this works

The judgment is based on newly received weather information.

Book example

その、しまったのならわたしにしてくれませんか。

If you have finished reading that book, could you lend it to me?

Why this works

のなら takes the completed action as the basis for a request.

2. ～ては…・～（の）では…

Formation

Vて／イAくて／ナAで／Nで + は／普通形 + のでは

Book explanation

The following clause is normally a negative evaluation, difficulty, or unwanted outcome rather than a hope, invitation, or request.

English study note

Core meaning: Use ～ては or ～のでは when the stated fact or assumed situation leads to an unfavorable result. Compare～なら can support advice or intention. ～ては／～のでは focuses on the bad consequence of the condition itself. Common mistakeDo not follow this pattern with the speaker's simple hope, invitation, or command.

Book examples

Book example

さんはしたばかりだから、おにってはかえってだろう。

Mr. Yamanaka has just had surgery, so visiting him would probably be more of a burden.

Why this works

Going to visit is evaluated as producing an undesirable result.

Book example

そんななではみんなにきらわれますよ。

With such an irresponsible attitude, everyone will dislike you.

Why this works

The attitude leads to a negative social consequence.

Book example

からをてのではには。

If construction starts only now, the house will not be finished within the year.

Why this works

The assumed late start makes completion impossible.

3. ～さえ～ば…・～さえ～なら…

Formation

N + さえ + Vば／イAければ／ナA・Nなら

Book explanation

さえ highlights the minimum requirement. Everything else is treated as less important once that one condition is met.

English study note

Core meaning: Use ～さえ～ば or ～さえ～なら to say that one condition alone is enough for the result. Compareだけ means only, but does not itself create a sufficient condition. さえ～ば explicitly means 'if only this condition is met.' Common mistakeThe predicate following さえ must take an appropriate conditional form such as ～ば or ～なら.

Book examples

Book example

はさえいればしないようだ。

Taro seems not to get bored as long as he can read manga.

Why this works

Manga is presented as the one sufficient minimum for avoiding boredom.

Book example

さえならどんなことにもできる。

As long as one is physically healthy, one can take on any challenge.

Why this works

Physical health alone is the sufficient condition.

Book example

「ごめんなさい。」といさえすれば、はくれるだろう。

If you only say one word, 'I'm sorry,' the other person will probably forgive you.

Why this works

言いさえすれば highlights the one action sufficient for forgiveness.

4. たとえ～ても…・たとえ～でも…

Formation

たとえ + Vても／イAくても／ナA・Nでも

Book explanation

たとえ adds emphasis and imagines a difficult, unlikely, or extreme case. The following clause refuses to change.

English study note

Core meaning: Use たとえ～ても or たとえ～でも to say the main result, decision, or judgment remains true even under an extreme condition. Compareても alone means even if. Adding たとえ makes the concession stronger and more deliberate. Common mistakeたとえ normally pairs with a concessive ending such as ～ても or ～でも, not a plain conditional.

Book examples

Book example

たとえのたちにどんなにされても、ぼくはプロのになりたい。

Even if everyone around me opposes it, I want to become a professional singer.

Why this works

たとえ pairs with ても to keep the goal unchanged despite opposition.

Book example

たとえても、になものはわなければならない。

Even if it is expensive, we must buy what is necessary for the job.

Why this works

The obligation remains even under the extreme condition of high cost.

Book example

たとえでも、はほうがいいですよ。

Even if it is troublesome, you should have a health examination every year.

Why this works

The recommendation remains despite the inconvenience.

5. ～ば…のに／～たら…のに／～なら…のに

Formation

Vば／Vたら／N・ナAなら + result + のに

Book explanation

The conditional identifies what would have made the desired result possible. のに signals that reality did not match that imagined outcome.

English study note

Core meaning: Use a conditional plus のに to imagine a result that differs from reality and express regret, disappointment, or an unreal wish. CompareWithout のに, these forms can state ordinary conditions. With のに, the sentence looks back or contrasts with reality. Common mistakeKeep the time relationship clear: ～たら often imagines a completed past condition; ～なら can imagine an identity or situation.

Book examples

Book example

おとひまがあればわたしもするんだけど……。

If I had money and free time, I would travel overseas too.

Why this works

あれば describes an unreal present condition and んだけど leaves the wish unfinished.

Book example

もししていたらこのにはれなかった。ってよかった。

If I had overslept, I could not have boarded this plane. I am glad I made it.

Why this works

ていたら imagines a past condition contrary to what actually happened.

Book example

ああ、だ。ならでチケットがえたのに……。

What a shame. If I were a student, I could have bought the ticket with a student discount.

Why this works

なら presents the unreal identity condition behind the regret.
