1. ～てもらいたい・～ていただきたい・～てほしい

Formation

／ + で + もらいたい・いただきたい・ほしい

Book explanation

～てもらいたい views the action as something you receive. ～ていただきたい is the respectful version. ～てほしい directly focuses on the other person's desired action. A negative request uses ～ないでほしい.

English study note

Core meaning: Express a wish that another person do something for you, or not do something that troubles you. Compare When speaking directly, these can function like requests, but they state your desire. ～てください directly asks the listener to act and is often more straightforward. Common mistake Keep the desired actor clear. In 友だちに来てほしい, the friend should come; in 友だちに来てもらいたい, the speaker wants to receive the friend's coming as a favor.

Book examples

Book example

だれかにのをいてもらいたいとうことがあります。

Sometimes I want someone to listen to my worries.

Why this works

The speaker wants to receive the act of listening from an unspecified person.

Book example

この、ちょっとていただきたいんですが。

I would like you to take a quick look at these documents.

Why this works

いただきたい respectfully presents the desired favor and んですが softens the request.

Book example

このはだれにもってもらいたくない。でやりたい。

I do not want anyone to help me with this job. I want to do it alone.

Why this works

The negative desire is attached to もらう: the speaker does not want to receive help.

Book example

ずっとぼくのそばにいてほしい。くへかないでほしい。

I want you to stay beside me. I do not want you to go far away.

Why this works

The first clause desires an action; the second desires that the listener refrain from leaving.

Book example

これこののをこわさないでほしい。

I want people to stop damaging this village's natural environment.

Why this works

The actor is understood generally, while ないでほしい expresses the desired restraint.

Book example

をったにはもうをしてほしくない。

I do not want my aging parents to overexert themselves anymore.

Why this works

ほしくない rejects the hoped-for action itself: the parents should not overdo things.

2. ～（さ）せてもらいたい・～（さ）せていただきたい・～（さ）せてほしい

Formation

の + もらいたい・いただきたい・ほしいのないで + もらいたい・いただきたい・ほしい

Book explanation

The speaker is normally the actor of the causative verb: 帰らせていただきたい means “I would like permission to leave.” The respectful いただきたい is common in workplace and service situations.

English study note

Core meaning: Express your wish to do something with another person's permission, or your wish not to be made to do something. Compare 見てもらいたい asks another person to look. 見させてもらいたい asks to be allowed to look yourself. The causative changes who performs the action. Common mistake Do not translate the causative mechanically as “make.” In this request pattern, it commonly means “allow me to.”

Book examples

Book example

A「がよね。をもっとべさせてもらいたいね。」B「そうだね。にってみよう。」

Clerk A: “Our lunch break is short. I wish we could be allowed to eat more slowly.” Clerk B: “Right. Let's talk to the manager.”

Why this works

The employees want permission and enough time to perform their own action.

Book example

はへかなければならないので、らせていただきたいのですが……。

I have to go to immigration today, so I would like permission to leave early.

Why this works

The employee respectfully asks the person in authority to allow an early departure.

Book example

それはさっきもしたことだよ。もことをわせないでもらいたいよ。

I already explained that. I do not want you to make me say the same thing repeatedly.

Why this works

The negative causative asks the other person not to create a situation that forces the speaker to repeat himself.

Book example

のポスターはわたしにらせてほしいなあ。

I wish they would let me make the cultural festival poster.

Why this works

に marks the speaker as the person who wants permission to perform the work.

Book example

こんなにで４もをさせないでほしいです。

I do not want them to make us practice on the field for four hours on such a hot day.

Why this works

The speaker wants the authority not to impose the unwanted practice.

3. ～といい・～ばいい・～たらいい

Formation

：（）+ といい／ + いい／たら + いい： + といい／・ + なければ + いい／たら + いい

Book explanation

For hope, the result is not under the speaker's direct control: weather, health, or examination results. For advice, the forms recommend an action. ～たらいい is conversational; ～ばいい can sound more matter-of-fact; ～といい presents a generally good course.

English study note

Core meaning: Express a hope about an outcome outside your control, or advise someone what action would be good. Compare A hope such as 晴れるといい does not order anyone to make the weather clear. Advice such as 早く寝るといい has a controllable human action. Common mistake For a simple wish, do not use a deliberate action by the speaker with といい. Say what outcome you hope will occur, not what you yourself have already decided to do.

Book examples

Book example

【で】「このもでおです。いつかまたみんなでえるといいですね。」

At graduation, the teacher says: “We part today. I hope we can all meet again someday.”

Why this works

The future reunion is a hoped-for outcome, not something the speaker can guarantee.

Book example

ずっとのがい。いでなければいいが……。

I have felt unwell lately. I hope it is not a serious illness.

Why this works

The negative conditional expresses hope about an uncertain diagnosis.

Book example

あしたはだ。がんばろう。できたらいいなあ。

The entrance exam is tomorrow. I will do my best. I hope I can pass.

Why this works

Passing is the desired result, but the final outcome remains uncertain.

Book example

れてようですね。あしたはといいですよ。

You look tired. You should rest well tomorrow.

Why this works

The controllable action 休む makes this advice rather than a wish.

Book example

その、のならきけなければいいんじゃないですか。

If you do not feel like doing that job, why not decline it?

Why this works

The negative conditional recommends not accepting the work.

Book example

のきがわからなければ、のにいてみたらいいですよ。

If you do not know how to fill out the application, you should ask the office staff.

Why this works

たらいい offers a practical next action in a conversational way.
