Want another person to act or refrain

1. ～てもらいたい・～ていただきたい・～てほしい

Core meaning: Express a wish that another person do something for you, or not do something that troubles you.

Formation

動て形／ない形 + で + もらいたい・いただきたい・ほしい

Nuance and use

～てもらいたい views the action as something you receive. ～ていただきたい is the respectful version. ～てほしい directly focuses on the other person's desired action. A negative request uses ～ないでほしい.

Compare

When speaking directly, these can function like requests, but they state your desire. ～てください directly asks the listener to act and is often more straightforward.

Common mistake

Keep the desired actor clear. In 友だちに来てほしい, the friend should come; in 友だちに来てもらいたい, the speaker wants to receive the friend's coming as a favor.

Examples

Book example

だれかに自分の悩みを聞いてもらいたいと思うことがあります。

Sometimes I want someone to listen to my worries.

Why this works

The speaker wants to receive the act of listening from an unspecified person.

Book example

この書類、ちょっと見ていただきたいんですが。

I would like you to take a quick look at these documents.

Why this works

いただきたい respectfully presents the desired favor and んですが softens the request.

Book example

この仕事はだれにも手伝ってもらいたくない。自分一人でやりたい。

I do not want anyone to help me with this job. I want to do it alone.

Why this works

The negative desire is attached to もらう: the speaker does not want to receive help.

Book example

ずっとぼくのそばにいてほしい。遠くへ行かないでほしい。

I want you to stay beside me. I do not want you to go far away.

Why this works

The first clause desires an action; the second desires that the listener refrain from leaving.

Book example

これ以上この村の自然環境をこわさないでほしい。

I want people to stop damaging this village's natural environment.

Why this works

The actor is understood generally, while ないでほしい expresses the desired restraint.

Book example

年を取った親にはもう無理をしてほしくない。

I do not want my aging parents to overexert themselves anymore.

Why this works

ほしくない rejects the hoped-for action itself: the parents should not overdo things.

会議の前に、この資料を読んでおいてもらいたいです。

I would like everyone to read this material before the meeting.

Why this works

The speaker wants to receive the group's preparation as a useful action.

先生に推薦状を書いていただきたいのですが。

I would like the professor to write me a recommendation letter.

Why this works

いただきたい suits a respectful request to a professor.

子どもたちには自分の意見を自由に話してほしい。

I want the children to express their own opinions freely.

Why this works

に marks the people whose action the speaker hopes for.

夜遅くに大きな音を出さないでほしい。

I want people not to make loud noises late at night.

Why this works

ないでほしい asks an understood actor to refrain from an unwanted action.

Guided checks

先生に作文を見て（　）のですが。

ここでたばこを吸わないで（　）。

友だちに引っ越しを手伝って（　）。

Recap: Choose もらいたい for a received favor, いただきたい for respectful distance, and ほしい when the desired action itself is central.

Ask to be allowed to act

2. ～（さ）せてもらいたい・～（さ）せていただきたい・～（さ）せてほしい

Core meaning: Express your wish to do something with another person's permission, or your wish not to be made to do something.

Formation

動詞の使役て形 + もらいたい・いただきたい・ほしい
動詞の使役ないで形 + もらいたい・いただきたい・ほしい

Nuance and use

The speaker is normally the actor of the causative verb: 帰らせていただきたい means “I would like permission to leave.” The respectful いただきたい is common in workplace and service situations.

Compare

見てもらいたい asks another person to look. 見させてもらいたい asks to be allowed to look yourself. The causative changes who performs the action.

Common mistake

Do not translate the causative mechanically as “make.” In this request pattern, it commonly means “allow me to.”

Examples

Book example

店員A「昼休みが短いよね。昼ご飯をもっとゆっくり食べさせてもらいたいね。」店員B「そうだね。店長に言ってみよう。」

Clerk A: “Our lunch break is short. I wish we could be allowed to eat more slowly.” Clerk B: “Right. Let's talk to the manager.”

Why this works

The employees want permission and enough time to perform their own action.

Book example

今日は入管へ行かなければならないので、早く帰らせていただきたいのですが……。

I have to go to immigration today, so I would like permission to leave early.

Why this works

The employee respectfully asks the person in authority to allow an early departure.

Book example

それはさっきも説明したことだよ。何度も同じことを言わせないでもらいたいよ。

I already explained that. I do not want you to make me say the same thing repeatedly.

Why this works

The negative causative asks the other person not to create a situation that forces the speaker to repeat himself.

Book example

文化祭のポスターはわたしに作らせてほしいなあ。

I wish they would let me make the cultural festival poster.

Why this works

に marks the speaker as the person who wants permission to perform the work.

Book example

こんな暑い日に運動場で４時間も練習をさせないでほしいです。

I do not want them to make us practice on the field for four hours on such a hot day.

Why this works

The speaker wants the authority not to impose the unwanted practice.

最初に、研究の目的を説明させていただきたいと思います。

First, I would like to explain the purpose of the research.

Why this works

This formal phrase politely claims the floor and asks permission to begin.

この企画はぜひ私に担当させてもらいたい。

I definitely want to be allowed to take charge of this project.

Why this works

The speaker requests permission to perform the role personally.

今日は体調が悪いので、先に休ませていただけませんか。

I am not feeling well today, so could you allow me to rest first?

Why this works

The causative plus respectful receiving frames rest as permission granted by the listener.

大切な発表なので、最後まで話させてほしい。

This presentation matters, so I want you to let me speak to the end.

Why this works

The speaker wants freedom to complete their own action.

子どもに危険な道を一人で歩かせないでほしい。

I do not want you to make the child walk the dangerous road alone.

Why this works

Here the child is the causative actor, and the speaker asks that this situation not be imposed.

Guided checks

今日は病院へ行くので、早く帰らせて（　）のですが。

私にもその写真を見（　）ほしい。

同じ説明を何度も（　）でほしい。

Recap: The causative identifies the action you want permission to perform—or the action you do not want someone to force.

Hope for an outcome or recommend an action

3. ～といい・～ばいい・～たらいい

Core meaning: Express a hope about an outcome outside your control, or advise someone what action would be good.

Formation

希望：普通形（現在形）+ といい／ば形 + いい／たら + いい
助言：動辞書形 + といい／ば形・ない形 + なければ + いい／たら + いい

Nuance and use

For hope, the result is not under the speaker's direct control: weather, health, or examination results. For advice, the forms recommend an action. ～たらいい is conversational; ～ばいい can sound more matter-of-fact; ～といい presents a generally good course.

Compare

A hope such as 晴れるといい does not order anyone to make the weather clear. Advice such as 早く寝るといい has a controllable human action.

Common mistake

For a simple wish, do not use a deliberate action by the speaker with といい. Say what outcome you hope will occur, not what you yourself have already decided to do.

Examples

Book example

【卒業式で】先生「このクラスも今日でお別れです。いつかまたみんなで会えるといいですね。」

At graduation, the teacher says: “We part today. I hope we can all meet again someday.”

Why this works

The future reunion is a hoped-for outcome, not something the speaker can guarantee.

Book example

最近ずっと体の調子が悪い。悪い病気でなければいいが……。

I have felt unwell lately. I hope it is not a serious illness.

Why this works

The negative conditional expresses hope about an uncertain diagnosis.

Book example

あしたは入学試験だ。がんばろう。合格できたらいいなあ。

The entrance exam is tomorrow. I will do my best. I hope I can pass.

Why this works

Passing is the desired result, but the final outcome remains uncertain.

Book example

疲れているようですね。あしたはゆっくり休むといいですよ。

You look tired. You should rest well tomorrow.

Why this works

The controllable action 休む makes this advice rather than a wish.

Book example

その仕事、気が進まないのなら引き受けなければいいんじゃないですか。

If you do not feel like doing that job, why not decline it?

Why this works

The negative conditional recommends not accepting the work.

Book example

申込書の書き方がわからなければ、事務の人に聞いてみたらいいですよ。

If you do not know how to fill out the application, you should ask the office staff.

Why this works

たらいい offers a practical next action in a conversational way.

旅行の日に雨が降らないといいですね。

I hope it does not rain on the day of the trip.

Why this works

The weather is outside the speakers' control, so this is a hope.

面接がうまくいけばいいなあ。

I hope the interview goes well.

Why this works

The conditional frames a desired but uncertain result.

道に迷ったら、駅員に聞いたらいいですよ。

If you get lost, you should ask a station attendant.

Why this works

The first たら sets the situation; the second recommends a useful action.

眠れないなら、温かい飲み物を飲むといい。

If you cannot sleep, it is good to drink something warm.

Why this works

といい presents a generally helpful course of action.

Guided checks

明日の試合に勝てる（　）なあ。

わからなければ、先生に聞い（　）ですよ。

病気で（　）いいが……。

Recap: First decide whether the sentence hopes for an uncontrollable result or recommends a controllable action.
