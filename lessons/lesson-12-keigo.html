Raise the person performing the action

1. 尊敬語（そんけいご）

Core meaning: Use respectful language for the actions or state of a customer, teacher, senior, or another person you honor.

Formation

お + Vます-stem + になる / ご + Sino-Japanese noun + になる; れる・られる; special respectful verbs

Nuance and use

Respectful language raises the subject. It never describes your own action. Choose the productive patterns or an established special verb such as いらっしゃる, なさる, or 召し上がる.

Compare

尊敬語 raises the other person's action; 謙譲語 lowers the speaker or in-group action in relation to that person.

Common mistake

Do not stack forms such as お帰りになられます. One respectful marker is normally enough: お帰りになります or 帰られます.

Lower an action directed toward a respected person

2. 謙譲語1（けんじょうご）

Core meaning: Humbly describe your action when it directly involves or benefits the person you respect.

Formation

お + Vます-stem + する / ご + Sino-Japanese noun + する; special humble verbs such as 伺う and 拝見する

Nuance and use

This group needs a relational target: you borrow from, guide, show, ask, visit, meet, or give to the respected person. That target may be omitted when obvious.

Compare

謙譲語1 has an honored target for the action. 謙譲語2 mainly makes the speaker's own behavior formally humble, even without such a target.

Common mistake

Do not use お／ご～する for an action that does not affect a respected target. Also avoid it for the respected person's action.

Examples

Book example

（の）ご本をお借りします。

I will borrow the teacher's book.

Present the speaker's action with formal humility

3. 謙譲語2（けんじょうご）

Core meaning: Use fixed humble verbs for the speaker or in-group, often in formal introductions, service, and business speech.

Formation

参る・おる・いたす・いただく・申す・存じる／存じておる

Nuance and use

These verbs lower the speaker's side and create a formal register. Unlike 謙譲語1, the action does not always point directly toward a respected person.

Compare

参る is the humble counterpart of 行く／来る; いらっしゃる is respectful and therefore belongs to the other person's action.

Common mistake

Do not use humble verbs for the customer or teacher: × 先生が参ります when you mean the teacher will come.

Examples

Book example

あした3時にます。

I will come at three tomorrow.

Ordinary form: 来る・行く

Why this works

The fixed humble verb presents the speaker or in-group action modestly in a formal setting. The book pairs it with 来る・行く, which makes the social-direction change explicit.

Book example

Make the whole statement formally polite

4. 丁寧語（ていねいご）

Core meaning: Use ございます and でございます as formal-polite versions of あります and です.

Formation

N + でございます; place + に + N + がございます

Nuance and use

丁寧語 raises the politeness of the utterance without specifically raising the subject or lowering the speaker. It is common in announcements, retail, hotels, and formal service encounters.

Compare

ございます is formal-polite, not automatically humble. おります is humble language for an animate speaker or in-group person.

Common mistake

Use ございます for things and locations. For the respectful presence of a person, use いらっしゃいます.

Examples

Book example

これはでございます。

This is a new product.

Ordinary form: ～だ

Why this works

The sentence uses formal-polite wording to elevate the register rather than the social status of a particular action. The book pairs it with ～だ, which makes the social-direction change explicit.
