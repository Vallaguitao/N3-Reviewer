1. 尊敬語（そんけいご）

Formation

お + Vます-stem + になる / ご + Sino-Japanese noun + になる; れる・られる; special respectful verbs

Book explanation

Respectful language raises the subject. It never describes your own action. Choose the productive patterns or an established special verb such as いらっしゃる, なさる, or 召し上がる.

English study note

Core meaning: Use respectful language for the actions or state of a customer, teacher, senior, or another person you honor. Compare尊敬語 raises the other person's action; 謙譲語 lowers the speaker or in-group action in relation to that person. Common mistakeDo not stack forms such as お帰りになられます. One respectful marker is normally enough: お帰りになります or 帰られます.

2. 謙譲語1（けんじょうご）

Formation

お + Vます-stem + する / ご + Sino-Japanese noun + する; special humble verbs such as 伺う and 拝見する

Book explanation

This group needs a relational target: you borrow from, guide, show, ask, visit, meet, or give to the respected person. That target may be omitted when obvious.

English study note

Core meaning: Humbly describe your action when it directly involves or benefits the person you respect. Compare謙譲語1 has an honored target for the action. 謙譲語2 mainly makes the speaker's own behavior formally humble, even without such a target. Common mistakeDo not use お／ご～する for an action that does not affect a respected target. Also avoid it for the respected person's action.

Book examples

Book example

（の）ごをおりします。

I will borrow the teacher's book.

Why this works

The speaker's action is lowered because it is directed toward or connected with the respected person. Ordinary form: （一般の動詞文）

3. 謙譲語2（けんじょうご）

Formation

参る・おる・いたす・いただく・申す・存じる／存じておる

Book explanation

These verbs lower the speaker's side and create a formal register. Unlike 謙譲語1, the action does not always point directly toward a respected person.

English study note

Core meaning: Use fixed humble verbs for the speaker or in-group, often in formal introductions, service, and business speech. Compare参る is the humble counterpart of 行く／来る; いらっしゃる is respectful and therefore belongs to the other person's action. Common mistakeDo not use humble verbs for the customer or teacher: × 先生が参ります when you mean the teacher will come.

Book examples

Book example

あした3にます。

I will come at three tomorrow.

Why this works

The fixed humble verb presents the speaker or in-group action modestly in a formal setting. Ordinary form: 来る・行く

4. 丁寧語（ていねいご）

Formation

N + でございます; place + に + N + がございます

Book explanation

丁寧語 raises the politeness of the utterance without specifically raising the subject or lowering the speaker. It is common in announcements, retail, hotels, and formal service encounters.

English study note

Core meaning: Use ございます and でございます as formal-polite versions of あります and です. Compareございます is formal-polite, not automatically humble. おります is humble language for an animate speaker or in-group person. Common mistakeUse ございます for things and locations. For the respectful presence of a person, use いらっしゃいます.

Book examples

Book example

これはでございます。

This is a new product.

Why this works

The sentence uses formal-polite wording to elevate the register rather than the social status of a particular action. Ordinary form: ～だ

Book example

は3にございます。

Computer supplies are on the third floor.

Why this works

The sentence uses formal-polite wording to elevate the register rather than the social status of a particular action. Ordinary form: ある
