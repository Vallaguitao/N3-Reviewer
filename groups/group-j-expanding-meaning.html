Book table A · action stage

ある動作のどの段階かを表す

Expresses the stage an action has reached.

Book overview

やにのをつけると、そのやのをことができます。

You can broaden the meaning of verbs and nouns by adding other words to them.

文法形式意味例文
～かける
～かけだ

～しいない

Indicates that an action is not complete.

ます ＋かける・かけだ

Move ＋Make/Kakeda

Book example

はかかけて、しまった。

She started to say something, but fell silent.

Book example

はのをおいて、に。

My mother put down the book she was reading and went shopping.

～きる

／に～する

Indicates completion of a task or action.

ます ＋きる

move + move

Book example

40キロのをきった。

I ran a long distance of 40km.

Book example

こんなにたくさん、ではきれない。

I can't eat this much by myself.

～通す

まで～し

Continue until completion, until the end.

ます ＋

move + pass

Book example

でことはまでやり。

I will follow through with whatever I decide to do.

Book example

はをも。

He remained silent no matter what was asked.

～出す

（・に）～し

Begin something suddenly.

ます ＋

move + put out

Book example

はわたしのをと、。

When he saw my face, he suddenly burst into laughter.

Book example

を、おもちゃのがに。

When I replaced the batteries, the toy car suddenly started moving.

Expanded explanation

かける stops partway, きる reaches the endpoint, 通す emphasizes persistence through the endpoint, and 出す marks a sudden beginning.

Added examples

Added example

かけのメールをした。

I saved the email I had started but not finished.

Added example

をできった。

I finished the long novel in one night.

Added example

はまでのを。

She maintained her opinion to the very end.

Added example

がに。

The baby suddenly began to cry.

Book table B · characteristics and condition

特徴・状態・様子を表す①

Expressing the characteristics, condition or appearance of something.

文法形式意味例文
～やすい

に～できる
に～してしまう

Means that something can happen, or is done, easily; “easy to ~,” or “it can easily ~.”

ます ＋やすい

Moves + Easy

Book example

のはわかりやすい。

Professor Tanaka's story is easy to understand.

Book example

やすいだからをつけて。

The paper is thin and easily tears, so be careful.

～にくい

に～できない
には～しない

Means that something cannot happen or be done easily; “not easy to ~,” or “it cannot easily ~.”

ます ＋にくい

Moves + Difficult

Book example

このはにくい。

This meat is tough and difficult to eat.

Book example

でにくいカップはありませんか。

Do you have a cup that is durable and won't break easily?

～づらい

～するのが

Means awkward or difficult to ~, causing the speaker discomfort.

ます ＋づらい

Moves + difficult

Book example

なことなのでのにはづらい。

Since it is a personal matter, it is difficult to ask someone at work.

Book example

ここはのがです。

This is a position where it is difficult to see the writing on the blackboard.

～すぎる
～すぎだ

なを
～だ・～する

Means to do too much, go too far in an action or process.

ます・イい・ナ ＋すぎる・すぎだ

move, i-shape, na-shape + too much, too much

Book example

このはわたしにはすぎる。

These shoes are too small for me.

Book example

おいしかったので、すぎてしまった。

It was so delicious that I ate too much.

Book example

、それはすぎだよ。

Man, that's too much.

Expanded explanation

にくい can describe objective resistance; づらい often adds the speaker’s physical, social, or emotional discomfort. やすい can be positive (“easy to understand”) or cautionary (“easy to tear”).

Added examples

Added example

このペンはやすい。

This pen is light and easy to write with.

Added example

このはがいてにくい。

These kanji look similar and are hard to remember.

Added example

にはそのをづらかった。

It was uncomfortable to tell the teacher about that failure.

Added example

このはでもやすい。

This road is easy to walk even at night.

Added example

がすぎてわからない。

The explanation is too technical to understand.

Added example

すぎないようにしてください。

Please take care not to work too much.

Book table B · tendency, image, resemblance, abundance

特徴・状態・様子を表す②

文法形式意味例文
～がちだ

～のになるがある

Means tends toward a state or situation.

・ます ＋がちだ

Formation: noun or verb stem + がちだ.

Book example

のためはだ。

Construction tends to be delayed due to rain.

Book example

ににをみた。

I hesitantly asked Yuka about her age.

～らしい

～のイメージとだ

Means conform with a preconceived image of, be typical of.

＋らしい

Name + seems to be

Book example

はらしいだった。

Today was a warm, spring-like day.

Book example

いつものなリーさんらしくないですね。

It doesn't look like the usual cheerful Lee.

～っぽい

～のような・がある

Have a certain nature or quality.

・ます ＋っぽい

Formation: noun or verb stem + っぽい.

Book example

はわがままばかり、っぽいだ。

She is selfish and childish.

Book example

はあきっぽくて、でやめてしまう。

My younger brother is easily distracted and always gives up halfway through everything.

～のようだ
～みたいだ

～によくいる

Be very similar to ~.

＋のようだ・みたいだ
＋のような・みたいな＋

Formation: noun + のようだ／みたいだ; noun + のような／みたいな + noun.

Book example

ここからはのようだ。

The view from here is like a painting.

Book example

このパンはケーキみたいに。

This bread is sweet and soft like a cake.

Book example

ガラスみたいなをで。

We played by stepping on the glass-like ice with our shoes.

～だらけだ

～（いやなもの）がたくさんある

Be in abundance, full of, littered with (usually used pejoratively).

＋だらけだ

Formation: noun + だらけだ.

Book example

きたテストはだった。

The test that came back was full of mistakes.

Book example

サッカーをしていたら、になった。

When I played soccer every day, I was covered in scars.

Expanded explanation

らしい says something fits its expected identity; っぽい says it merely has a noticeable quality. ようだ／みたいだ makes a comparison, while だらけ usually evaluates an unwanted abundance.

Added examples

Added example

とをがちだ。

When busy, I tend to skip breakfast.

Added example

はらしいだ。

Today is a cool day typical of autumn.

Added example

このスープは。

This soup is a little watery.

Added example

あのはのように。

That cloud looks like an animal.

Added example

はだった。

The station was packed with tourists.

Added example

のはっぽく。

His explanation sounded rather excuse-like.

One-point lesson · source comparison

「～らしい」と「～のようだ・～みたいだ」

どちらが適当な方を選びなさい。

1　もう大人なんだから、そんな子ども（　）しゃべり方はやめろ。

2　子どもにはやはり子ども（　）服が似合いますよね。

3　6月に入ってからまだ雨（　）雨は降っていない。

4　あれ、雪（　）雨が降ってきたよ。

5　まだ5月なのに、今日は夏（　）日でしたね。

6　へえ、これが交番か。絵本に出てくるかわいい家（　）ね。

7　箱根は昔からの観光地だから、観光地（　）お土産屋が多い。

Book distinction

～らしい

に～だ。～のイメージとだ。

Means something is as it is supposed to be, as imagined.

Book example

はらしいだった。（はだ。）

It was spring-like weather today. (It really is spring now.)

Book example

はおらしいにぎやかさだった。（におだ。）

The town was lively, as if it were a festival. (It's actually a festival.)

～のようだ・～みたいだ

に～ではないが、～によくいる。「～みたいだ」は。

Means not actually ~, but is very similar to ~. ～みたいだ is used in spoken language.

Book example

はのようなだった。（はではない。）

The weather today was like spring. (It's not spring now.)

Book example

はおみたいなにぎやかさだった。（にはおではない。）

The town was lively, like a festival. (It's not actually a festival.)
