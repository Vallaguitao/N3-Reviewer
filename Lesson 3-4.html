第3課

比べれば…〜がいちばん

INSTRUMENT CLASS: DEGREE GAUGE

All four patterns here are instruments for measuring degree: ① illustrate how much with a vivid example, ② peg the needle at "nothing exceeds this," ③ mark a rock-bottom line with "if it comes to that," ④ point at the top of the scale with "this is the best." What they share: the reading is always the speaker's subjective judgment, never objective fact — the textbook repeats this warning for every pattern.

3-1

〜くらいだ／〜ぐらいだ／〜ほどだ

so … that / to the extent that …
EXTENT
接続名・動・形 普通形（ナ形 だ→な）＋ くらい(だ)／ほど(だ)

Shows how extreme something is by painting a concrete, vivid example of what it feels like. The key: the example did not actually happen. 「プールに飛び込みたいくらい暑い」 means "so hot I could almost dive into a pool" — the almost is the whole point. You are measuring the heat with an image, not reporting an event. The set phrase 〜かと思うくらい／ほど ("so … you'd think …") is a common variant.

EXAMPLES — tap to reveal EN

今日は暑あつくて、プールに飛とび込こみたいくらいだ。

It's so hot today I feel like diving into a pool.※ Didn't actually dive in — the image just measures the heat.

その映画えいがは感動的かんどうてきで、涙なみだが出でるほどだった。

The movie was so moving it nearly brought me to tears.※ ほど feels slightly more formal/written than くらい.

彼かれの日本語にほんごは上手じょうずで、日本人にほんじんかと思おもうくらいだ。

His Japanese is so good you'd think he was a native speaker.※ 「〜かと思うくらい／ほど」= "so … that you'd think …" — a set pattern from the textbook.

疲つかれて、もう一歩いっぽも歩あるけないほどだった。

I was so exhausted I couldn't take another step.※ Negative potential + ほど = a classic "peak exhaustion" reading.

祖母そぼの作つくるカレーは辛からくて、口くちから火ひが出でるくらいだ。

My grandma's curry is so spicy it's like fire coming out of my mouth.※ Physically impossible image = pure degree-illustration.

⚠ CALIBRATION NOTES

① だ can drop when modifying a noun: 泣なきたいくらいうれしい (so happy I could cry).

② くらい and ぐらい are interchangeable here; ほど is a touch more formal. All measure the same thing.

3-2

〜くらい…はない／〜ほど…はない

nothing is as … as ～ (subjective superlative)
MAXIMUM
接続名 ＋ くらい／ぐらい／ほど ＋ …はない・…はいない

This pattern pegs the needle at maximum: 「Xくらい〜なものはない」 = X is the most ~ of all. But as the textbook stresses, this is the speaker's subjective judgment, not objective fact. 「富士山ほど美しい山はない」 is my opinion, not a survey result — and voicing a personal superlative is exactly what this pattern is for: "~ is the most … in my book."

EXAMPLES — tap to reveal EN

富士山ふじさんほど美うつくしい山やまはないと思おもう。

I think no mountain is as beautiful as Mt. Fuji.※ と思う makes the subjectivity explicit — a natural pairing.

母ははの手料理てりょうりくらいおいしいものはない。

Nothing is as delicious as my mom's home cooking.※ Clearly opinion, not fact — exactly what this pattern is for.

朝あさの満員まんいん電車でんしゃほど嫌いやなものはない。

Nothing is as unpleasant as a packed morning train.※ Strong personal feeling — the pattern's home territory.

健康けんこうほど大切たいせつなものはない。

Nothing is as precious as your health.※ Sounds proverbial; still framed as the speaker's judgment.

彼女かのじょくらい練習れんしゅうに熱心ねっしんな学生がくせいはいない。

No student is as dedicated to practice as she is.※ For people, the negative is はいない, not はない.

⚠ CALIBRATION NOTES

① People take 〜はいない; things take 〜はない. ✗彼ほど親切な人はない → ○彼ほど親切な人はいない。

② For objective facts, use plain superlatives instead: 「日本でいちばん高たかい山は富士山だ」(fact) vs 「富士山ほどきれいな山はない」(opinion).

3-3

〜くらいなら…／〜ぐらいなら…

rather than (stoop to) ～, better to …
AVERSION
接続動 辞書形 ＋ くらいなら／ぐらいなら（＋ 〜ほうがいい／〜ほうがましだ）

"Compared to the rock-bottom scenario of doing X, Y would still be better" — the speaker strongly dislikes X. It typically pairs with 〜ほうがいい or 〜ほうがましだ; ましだ means "the lesser of two evils" — both options are bad, but Y is less bad. Another subjective-judgment pattern.

EXAMPLES — tap to reveal EN

途中とちゅうでやめるくらいなら、最初さいしょから始はじめないほうがいい。

If you're going to quit halfway, better not to start at all.※ Quitting halfway = the despised option X.

満員まんいん電車でんしゃで通勤つうきんするくらいなら、一時間いちじかん早はやく起おきて歩あるく。

Rather than commute on a packed train, I'll get up an hour earlier and walk.※ The main clause can also be a plain declaration of the alternative.

うそをつくくらいなら、何なにも言いわないほうがましだ。

Better to say nothing at all than to tell a lie.※ ましだ: both are bad, silence is less bad.

あの人ひとに頼たのむくらいなら、自分じぶんでやったほうがいい。

I'd rather do it myself than ask that person.※ Implies strong reluctance toward asking them.

まずい料理りょうりにお金かねを払はらうくらいなら、何なにも食たべないほうがましだ。

Rather than pay for bad food, I'd sooner eat nothing.※ Exaggerated aversion is natural with this pattern.

⚠ CALIBRATION NOTES

① This one is くらい only: ✗やめるほどなら → ○やめるくらいなら。The gauge pair breaks here.

② Attaches to the dictionary form of verbs. X must be something the speaker finds clearly undesirable.

3-4

〜に限かぎる

～ is the best / nothing beats ～
OPTIMUM
接続名／動 辞書形・ない形 ＋ に限る

States the speaker's verdict: "~ is the best" / "~ is the best method（方法ほうほう）." It carries a from-experience, "trust me, nothing beats it" flavor, so it shows up constantly in advice and personal mottos. Once again a subjective judgment, not objective fact — the unifying theme of every Lesson 3 pattern.

EXAMPLES — tap to reveal EN

疲つかれた日ひは、早はやく寝ねるに限る。

On exhausting days, nothing beats going to bed early.※ Verb dictionary form + に限る, no の in between.

夏なつはやっぱり冷つめたいビールに限る。

In summer, nothing beats a cold beer.※ やっぱり pairs naturally — "as I always say…"

風邪かぜをひいたときは、無理むりをしないに限る。

When you catch a cold, the best thing is not to push yourself.※ ない形 works too: "not doing X is best."

ストレス解消かいしょうには、カラオケに限る。

For stress relief, karaoke is the way to go.※ Noun attaches directly.

迷まよったときは、経験者けいけんしゃに相談そうだんするに限る。

When in doubt, the best move is to ask someone with experience.※ Classic advice-giving tone.

⚠ CALIBRATION NOTES

① Don't confuse with lookalikes: 〜に限って (「うちの子に限って…」 "my child, of all people, would never…") and 〜とは限らない ("not necessarily") are different grammar with different meanings.

② に限る states your verdict; 〜がいちばんいい states a comparison result. They overlap, but に限る sounds like a personal motto.

較正表①　くらい ⇄ ほど

WHEN ARE THEY INTERCHANGEABLE?
PatternくらいほどNotes
Degree illustration（〜くらいだ）○○ほど is slightly more formal / written
Superlative（〜…はない）○○Both fine; both subjective
〜なら (vs a hated option)○ くらいなら✗ ほどならくらい only
AはBほど〜ない (plain comparison)✗○"A is not as ~ as B" — ほど only
Trivial degree ("at least ~")○ あいさつくらいしなさい✗Trivial-degree くらい ("at least")

Rule of thumb: overlapping in the middle of the gauge, but the extremes split — low/trivial degree → くらい, plain comparison & formal writing → ほど.

第4課

〜とは違ちがって

INSTRUMENT CLASS: CONTRAST BALANCE

The five patterns here are all contrast balances — but each puts something different on the scale: ① weighs two separate things against each other, ②③ examine two faces of one and the same thing, ④ re-labels a way of saying something, ⑤ marks an exchange or substitution. Telling ①–③ apart is one of the biggest trap zones in all of N3.

4-1

〜に対たいして…

in contrast to ～ (two different things)
A ⇄ B
接続普通形（ナ形 -な／-である・名 -な／-である）＋ のに対して　／　名 ＋ に対して

Clearly contrasts two separate things (A vs B), setting them face to face — Tokyo vs Osaka, older brother vs younger brother, Japan vs the Philippines. Picture different objects on each pan of the balance. The telltale sign: the sentence has two subjects. Best suited to relatively objective contrasts anyone could observe.

EXAMPLES — tap to reveal EN

兄あにがスポーツが得意とくいなのに対して、弟おとうとは音楽おんがくが得意だ。

While my older brother is good at sports, my younger brother is good at music.※ Two different people = に対して territory.

東京とうきょうの冬ふゆは乾燥かんそうしているのに対して、日本海側にほんかいがわは雪ゆきが多おおい。

Winters in Tokyo are dry, whereas the Sea of Japan side gets heavy snow.※ Two regions, objectively contrasted.

日本にほんでは車くるまは左側ひだりがわを走はしるのに対して、フィリピンでは右側みぎがわを走る。

In Japan cars drive on the left, whereas in the Philippines they drive on the right.※ A contrast you already know from experience.

姉あねが静しずかな性格せいかくであるのに対して、妹いもうとはとてもにぎやかだ。

My older sister has a quiet personality, whereas my younger sister is very lively.※ Note the formal -である connection for ナ形/名.

先月せんげつは雨あめが多かったのに対して、今月こんげつはほとんど降ふっていない。

Last month it rained a lot; this month it has barely rained at all.※ それに対して can also start a new sentence: 「…。それに対して、…」

⚠ CALIBRATION NOTES

① Don't forget the の: ✗得意なに対して → ○得意なのに対して。(Nouns attach directly: 兄に対して.)

② に対して has a second, unrelated meaning: "toward / regarding" a target（お客きゃくさんに対して失礼しつれいだ）. This lesson covers only the contrast meaning — read the sentence to decide which one you're seeing.

4-2

〜反面はんめん…

～, but on the other hand … (two sides of ONE thing)
ONE COIN
接続普通形（ナ形 -な／-である・名 -である）＋ 反面

Describes the two opposing sides — usually a plus and a minus — of one and the same thing. Not a balance scale but one coin, two faces: "city life" is a single topic that is both interesting (front) and stressful (back). The telltale sign: only one subject.

EXAMPLES — tap to reveal EN

都会とかいの生活せいかつは便利べんりな反面、お金かねがかかる。

City life is convenient, but on the flip side, it's expensive.※ One topic (city life), plus side + minus side.

この仕事しごとは給料きゅうりょうがいい反面、残業ざんぎょうが多おおい。

This job pays well, but on the other hand there's a lot of overtime.※ The classic trade-off sentence — perfect 反面 territory.

ネットショッピングは楽らくな反面、失敗しっぱいも多おおい。

Online shopping is easy, but it also comes with a lot of misses.※ ナ形 + な + 反面.

彼かれは厳きびしい反面、優やさしいところもある。

He's strict, but he also has a kind side.※ Same person, two contrasting traits.

一人暮ひとりぐらしは自由じゆうな反面、寂さびしいこともある。

Living alone is freeing, but on the other hand it can get lonely.※ Both faces of the same lifestyle.

⚠ CALIBRATION NOTES

① 反面 needs one subject with two aspects. Two different subjects → use に対して instead: ✗兄は背せが高い反面、弟は低い → ○兄が背が高いのに対して、弟は低い。

② The two aspects usually pull in opposite directions (good ⇄ bad). Two merits or two flaws sound odd with 反面.

4-3

〜一方いっぽう（で）…

while / whereas / at the same time …
PARALLEL
接続普通形（ナ形 -な／-である・名 -である）＋ 一方（で）

The contrast pattern with the widest coverage. It handles ① two opposing aspects (≈ 反面) and ② two things happening in parallel at the same time — 「働く一方で、勉強もしている」 is not a conflict, just "doing B alongside A," which 反面 cannot express. Very frequent in written and news-style Japanese.

EXAMPLES — tap to reveal EN

彼かれは会社かいしゃで働はたらく一方で、大学院だいがくいんでも勉強べんきょうしている。

He works at a company while also studying in grad school.※ Not a conflict — two parallel activities. 反面 can't do this.

物価ぶっかが上あがる一方で、給料きゅうりょうはなかなか上がらない。

Prices keep rising, while salaries barely rise at all.※ News-style contrast between two trends.

スマホは便利べんりな一方で、目めが悪わるくなる原因げんいんにもなる。

Smartphones are convenient, but at the same time they can damage your eyesight.※ Here it's interchangeable with 反面.

甘あまいものが好すきな人ひとがいる一方で、苦手にがてな人もいる。

Some people love sweets, while others can't stand them.※ Two groups contrasted — closer to に対して's job, and 一方 handles it fine.

会議かいぎでは自分じぶんの意見いけんを言いう一方で、ほかの人ひとの話はなしもよく聞きいてください。

In meetings, share your own opinions, but also listen carefully to others.※ "Do both" — the parallel/simultaneous use again.

⚠ CALIBRATION NOTES

① Sentence-final 〜一方だ is different grammar: 「人口じんこうは減へる一方だ」= "keeps on decreasing" (one-way trend). Position tells you which one you're reading.

② When the meaning is "two opposing aspects," 一方（で） ≒ 反面 and both work（教科書の③④参照）. When the meaning is "doing B alongside A," only 一方（で） works.

4-4

〜というより…

rather than (calling it) ～, it's more like …
RE-LABEL
接続比較のために取とり上あげる言葉ことば（そのままの形かたち）＋ というより

Compares not two things but two ways of putting it: "rather than calling it X, Y is the more accurate description." Picture peeling off a label and sticking on a better one — "he's not so much a singer as a comedian." You re-measure, then re-label to the truer mark.

EXAMPLES — tap to reveal EN

彼かれは歌手かしゅというより、芸人げいにんみたいだ。

He's less a singer than a comedian, really.※ Correcting the label 歌手 → 芸人.

この部屋へやは涼すずしいというより、寒さむいくらいだ。

This room isn't so much cool as downright cold.※ Bonus: combines with Lesson 3's 〜くらいだ.

あの二人ふたりは夫婦ふうふというより、友ともだちのように見みえる。

Those two seem more like friends than a married couple.※ Often paired with みたいだ／ようだ.

歩あるいていたというより、走はしっていたと言いったほうがいいスピードだった。

At that speed, "running" would be more accurate than "walking."※ Any word/phrase can be taken up as-is for re-labeling.

それは注意ちゅういというより、命令めいれいだった。

That wasn't so much a warning as an order.※ Echoes the textbook's 頼まれたというより命令された.

⚠ CALIBRATION NOTES

① Plain より compares two things（バスより電車のほうが速い）; というより compares two ways of putting it. If you can insert "calling it" in English, it's というより.

② The corrected label comes after: X というより Y ＝ Y is the better description.

4-5

〜かわりに…

in exchange for ～ / instead of ～
EXCHANGE
接続動・形 普通形（ナ形 -な）＋ かわりに　／　名 ＋ の ＋ かわりに

Two uses. ① Exchange / compensation: "X is true, but in return there is Y of roughly equal weight" — a minus balanced out by a plus. ② Substitution: "skip the usual X and do Y of equivalent value instead." Either way the core idea is balance — things trading off evenly.

EXAMPLES — tap to reveal EN

このアパートは駅えきから遠とおいかわりに、家賃やちんが安やすい。

This apartment is far from the station, but in exchange the rent is cheap.※ Use ① — a minus balanced by a plus.

父ちちは平日へいじつ忙いそがしいかわりに、週末しゅうまつは家族かぞくと過すごすようにしている。

My dad is busy on weekdays, but to make up for it he spends weekends with the family.※ Compensation — paying back the minus.

日本語にほんごを教おしえてもらうかわりに、英語えいごを教えてあげている。

In exchange for being taught Japanese, I teach them English.※ A clean two-way trade — like a language exchange partner.

今年ことしは旅行りょこうに行いくかわりに、新あたらしいパソコンを買かうことにした。

Instead of traveling this year, I decided to buy a new computer.※ Use ② — skip the usual thing, do something of equal value.

電話でんわするかわりに、メールで連絡れんらくした。

Instead of calling, I got in touch by email.※ Substitution of one action for an equivalent one.

⚠ CALIBRATION NOTES

① かわりに vs 反面: both can frame plus/minus, but かわりに implies exchange / balancing out; 反面 just observes two sides with no sense of trade.

② Nouns need の: ○バターのかわりにマーガリンを使う。

較正表②　The Contrast Family — Telling Them Apart

に対して × 反面 × 一方（で） × かわりに — WHICH BALANCE?
PatternWhat goes on the scaleNuanceQuick test
〜に対してTwo separate things (A vs B)Objective, clear-cut contrastTwo subjects in the sentence? → ○
〜反面Two faces of one thingPlus side ⇄ minus sideOne thing's merit AND flaw? → ○
〜一方（で）Either of the above ＋ parallel actionsWritten / news register, widest rangeAlso works for "doing B alongside A"
〜かわりにA balanced exchange"In return" — a sense of tradeTranslatable as "trade / make up for"? → ○

When stuck, count the subjects: two separate things → に対して; one thing's two sides → 反面; either / simultaneous actions → 一方で; a sense of trade → かわりに. The classic exam trap is making you choose between 反面 and に対して.
