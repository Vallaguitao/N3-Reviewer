Lesson 1 · 第1課
時

Time & timing

Every pattern here pins one event onto a stretch of time. The trick is always where on the timeline the second thing lands — at the start, somewhere in the middle, or right at the edge.

L1 · pattern 1
〜うちに
while X still holds — do it before the window closes

The feeling that separates うちに from a plain "while": the state won't last, so act now. There are two flavors.

明るいうちに — the bright window is open now, but closing
明るい（state holds）
✕ 暗くなる
act inside the window →
Connection
名 + の動 dict / ている / ないイ いナ な+うちに
Flavor A — intentional: I do something before it changes
日本にほんにいるうちに、もっと漢字かんじを覚おぼえたい。
While I'm in Japan, I want to learn more kanji. (the chance won't last)
温あたたかいうちに、どうぞ召めし上あがってください。
Please eat while it's still warm.
忘わすれないうちに、メモしておこう。
Let me jot it down before I forget. (negative state: while "not yet forgotten" holds)
Flavor B — unintentional: a change creeps in on its own
動 dict / ている / ない+うちに
勉強べんきょうしているうちに、だんだん日本語にほんごが好すきになった。
As I kept studying, I gradually came to like Japanese. (no one decided this)
音楽おんがくを聞きいているうちに、眠ねむってしまった。
While listening to music, I drifted off to sleep.
L1 · pattern 2 — the big one
〜間あいだ ／ 〜間あいだに
during a period — but throughout vs. at one point within

Same period, one tiny に, totally different meaning. This is the distinction the test loves.

Period: 赤あかちゃんが寝ねている
間 — my action fills the WHOLE period
赤ちゃんが寝ている（period）
ずっと本ほんを読よんでいた
間に — my action happens at ONE moment inside
家事を済ませた
Connection
名 + の動 dict / ている / ない+間 / 間に
間 = the whole time
母ははが料理りょうりを作つくっている間、わたしは宿題しゅくだいをしていた。
The entire time mom cooked, I was doing homework. (both continuous)
vs
間に = one moment within
母ははが買かい物ものに行いっている間に、こっそりケーキを食たべた。
While mom was out shopping, I snuck a slice of cake. (one quick act)
Rule of thumb: if the second verb is also a continuous action (〜ていた, ずっと), use 間. If it's a one-shot action that finishes (済ませた, 食べた, 出かけた), use 間に.
L1 · pattern 3
〜てからでないと ／ 〜てからでなければ
unless X is done first, Y can't happen

X is a gate. Until you pass through it, Y is blocked. The back half is always negative — can't, won't, mustn't, impossible.

X してから
→
そうしないと Y はできない
Connection
動 て形+からでないと / からでなければ
よく考かんがえてからでないと、返事へんじはできません。
I can't give an answer unless I think it over carefully first.
準備じゅんび運動うんどうをしてからでないと、泳およいではいけない。
You mustn't swim unless you warm up first.
親おやの許可きょかをもらってからでなければ、行いけない。
I can't go unless I get my parents' permission first.
パスポートを見みせてからでないと、中なかに入はいれません。
You can't go inside unless you show your passport first.
Watch for the negative tail. If your sentence ends positively (〜できる, 〜していい), this pattern is the wrong choice — you probably want plain 〜てから instead.
L1 · pattern 4
〜ところだ
pinpoints the exact phase of an action — and the verb form sets which

One pattern, three timestamps. The form in front of ところ slides you along the action's timeline.

The action: 出でかける (going out)
dict 出かけるabout to
ている 出かけているmid-action
た 出かけたjust did
Connection
動 dict（about to）ている（in progress）た（just finished）+ところ + だ/に/へ/を…
今いまから出でかけるところです。
dict → about to: I'm just about to head out now.
今いま、夕飯ゆうはんを作つくっているところです。
ている → in progress: I'm in the middle of making dinner right now.
今いま、駅えきに着ついたところです。
た → just finished: I just this second arrived at the station.
The particle shifts with the next verb. ところを見みられた (was seen mid-act), ところに電話でんわが鳴なった (phone rang right then), ところへ帰かえってきた (came home right then). The だ just becomes に/へ/を to fit.
Lesson 2 · 第2課
関

Relation & correlation

These link two things: one mirrors another, varies with another, repeats with another, or scales with another. Less about time, more about how A and B move together.

L2 · pattern 1
〜とおり(に) ／ 〜どおり(に)
exactly as ~ / the same way as ~

B happens in perfect conformity with A — a model you copy or a prediction that comes true. とおり after verbs/の, どおり when it sticks straight onto a noun.

Connection
動 dict / た / てある / ている名 + の+とおり(に)·名+どおり(に)
わたしが言いったとおりにやってください。
Do it exactly as I said.
予想よそうしたとおり、試験しけんは難むずかしかった。
Just as I predicted, the exam was hard.
説明書せつめいしょに書かいてあるとおりに組くみ立たてた。
I assembled it the way the manual describes.
結果けっかは想像そうぞうどおりだった。
The result was just as I'd imagined. (noun + どおり)
L2 · pattern 2
〜によって ／ 〜によっては
it depends — outcome varies with the noun

Two shades you must keep apart: plain によって says "results differ across the whole range," while によっては zooms in on one possible case from that range.

によって — varies broadly
国くにによって習慣しゅうかんが違ちがう。
Customs differ from country to country.
vs
によっては — one case
天気てんきによっては中止ちゅうしになる。
Depending on the weather (in some cases), it'll be cancelled.
More reps
感かんじ方かたは人ひとによってさまざまだ。
How people feel about it varies from person to person.
時間帯じかんたいによって電車でんしゃの混こみ方かたが違ちがう。
How crowded the trains are differs by time of day.
場合ばあいによっては、予定よていを変更へんこうすることもある。
Depending on the situation, I sometimes change my plans.
Heads-up for later: によって has two more jobs you'll meet at N3 — "by means of" (メールによって 連絡れんらくする) and the agent in passive (ピカソによって 描えがかれた). Same word, context tells you which.
L2 · pattern 3
〜たびに
every single time X happens, Y happens too

Stresses that it's always, without exception. Reserve it for noteworthy repeats — not boring everyday routine (don't use it for "every time I breathe").

X→Y X→Y X→Y … every time, no exceptions
Connection
名 + の動 dict+たびに
この写真しゃしんを見みるたびに、留学りゅうがくしていたころを思おもい出だす。
Every time I look at this photo, I think back to my study-abroad days.
彼かれに会あうたびに、髪型かみがたが変かわっている。
Every time I see him, his hairstyle has changed.
試験しけんのたびに緊張きんちょうして眠ねむれなくなる。
Every time there's an exam, I get nervous and can't sleep. (noun + の omitted → の試験のたびに)
L2 · pattern 4
(〜ば)〜ほど ／ 〜ほど
the more X, the more Y — two dials turning together

As one quantity climbs, the other climbs (or falls) in step. The classic shape repeats the word: 考えれば考えるほど.

Connection
動 ば形+動 dict·イ ければ+イ い·ナ なら+ナ な+ほど
日本語にほんごは勉強べんきょうすれば勉強べんきょうするほど面白おもしろくなる。
The more you study Japanese, the more interesting it gets. (verb)
考かんがえれば考かんがえるほど、わからなくなる。
The more I think about it, the less I understand. (more X → less Y works too)
値段ねだんが高たかければ高たかいほどいい、というわけではない。
More expensive doesn't necessarily mean better. (い-adjective)
忙いそがしい人ひとほど時間じかんの使つかい方かたが上手じょうずだ。
The busier a person is, the better they manage their time. (noun + ほど, no ば)
L2 · pattern 5
〜ついでに
while you're already doing X, take the chance to also do Y

X is the real reason you're out/up/busy; Y is a bonus errand you piggyback on the opportunity. Y is secondary — and the two needn't happen at the exact same instant.

X（main purpose）
+ Y（bonus）
Connection
名する + の動 dict / た+ついでに
買かい物もののついでに、郵便局ゆうびんきょくに寄よってきた。
While I was out shopping, I dropped by the post office too. (noun + の)
洗濯せんたくするついでに、シーツも洗あらった。
While doing the laundry, I washed the sheets as well. (verb dict)
コンビニに行いくついでに、ゴミを出だしてくれる？
Since you're heading to the convenience store anyway, could you take out the trash?
ついでに ≠ ながら. ながら = two actions at the very same time (歩あるきながら話はなす, talk while walking). ついでに = you exploit the opportunity of doing X to fit in Y; Y can come a moment later, and Y is the lesser errand.
The pairs that get mixed up

Confusion-killers

If two patterns feel interchangeable, here's the one line that splits them.

間 vs 間に

Second verb continuous (ずっと…ていた) → 間. Second verb one-shot (…した and done) → 間に.

うちに vs 間に

Both can mean "while," but うちに adds urgency — do it before this window closes / before it changes. 間に is neutral timing with no "act now" pressure.

ついでに vs ながら

ながら = simultaneous, both actions equal. ついでに = Y rides along on the opportunity of X; Y is secondary and can lag slightly.

によって vs によっては

No は → "varies across the board" (国によって違う). With は → "in certain cases" (場合によっては中止), highlighting one outcome from the range.

Your kanji game-plan

Recognition, not handwriting

You're right to skip writing — the JLPT never tests it. But "look it up on the go" alone won't stick. Here's the version that works.

1 · Learn words, not characters

A lone kanji has too many readings to "know." 生 only makes sense inside 学生がくせい・生うまれる・先生せんせい. Study N3 vocabulary and the kanji ride along — in the exact form the test uses.

2 · One SRS layer

Every on-the-go lookup → drop it in Anki. Ten minutes of review a day does the remembering for you. Looking up isn't learning; reviewing is.

3 · Read to recognize

Your 読解 book is a kanji gym. Don't pre-study every character — read, let context carry you, look up only what blocks meaning. That's exactly how kanji shows up on test day.

⚠ Mind the gap

N5 kanji + N3 grammar is a real gap — N3 reading needs ~650 recognized. It won't close by drifting, but step 1 closes it as a side effect of vocab study. And your own quiz tooling + JFT dataset can become a recognition drill.
