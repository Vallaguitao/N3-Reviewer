Book source · pattern 1

普通形＋と…「言う・思う・感じる」など

Book formation

＋と…「・・」など

Ordinary form + and… “say, think, feel” etc.

Expanded explanation

How the sentence is built

Put the quoted thought or statement into plain form before と. The final reporting verb carries the tense and politeness for the whole sentence.

Common mistake

Do not keep でしょう inside the embedded clause when the surrounding report requires the plain equivalent だろう.

Book examples

Book example

わたしは。＋「リンさんはきっとするでしょう。」

I think so. ＋``I'm sure Mr. Rin will pass the exam.''

→

わたしはリンさんはきっとするだろうと。

I think Mr. Lin will definitely pass the exam.

Added examples

Added example

はと。

Mr. Tanaka said that he would come tomorrow.

Why this works

The quoted content 明日来る is plain before と言いました.

Added example

このがいちばんだと。

I think this method is the safest.

Why this works

A noun or な-adjective statement uses だ inside a plain quotation.

Book source · pattern 2

「辞書形・ない形」＋ように（＋と）／「て形・ない形＋で」＋ほしい＋と／命令形・禁止形＋と

Book formation

「・ない」＋ように（＋と）／［て・ない＋で］＋ほしい＋と／・＋と　…「・する・」など

“Dictionary form/nai form” + yo ni (+ to) / [te form/nai form + with] + want + to/imperative form/prohibited form + and… “to ask, to be careful, to say”, etc.

Expanded explanation

How the sentence is built

Use ように for an indirect instruction, てほしい for the action you want another person to perform, and the imperative or prohibitive when reporting a direct command.

Common mistake

Keep the direction of the request clear: the person marked by に is the person asked, advised, or ordered to act.

Book examples

Book example

はカンさんに。＋「ときどきをしたほうがいいです」

The doctor recommended it to Mr. Kang. ＋"It's good to exercise sometimes."

→

はカンさんにときどきをするように。

The doctor recommended that Mr. Kang exercise from time to time.

Book example

わたしはに。＋「してくださいませんか」

I asked the teacher. + “Could you please explain it to me again?”

→

わたしはにしてほしいと。

I asked the teacher to explain it again.

Book example

はわたしに。＋「をになさいね」

my mother said to me. ＋"Please use your money wisely"

→

はわたしにをにと。

My mother told me to use my money wisely.

Added examples

Added example

はにをくるように。

The teacher told the students to bring a dictionary.

Why this works

ように言う reports an instruction without using a direct imperative.

Added example

にほしいと。

I asked the manager to let me take next week off.

Why this works

てほしいと頼む states the action requested from the manager.

Book source · pattern 3

普通形＋かどうか／疑問詞＋～か

Book formation

＋かどうか／＋～か　…「・わからない・」など

Ordinary form + whether or not / interrogative word + ~ or… “listen, don’t understand, investigate” etc.

Expanded explanation

How the sentence is built

Use かどうか for a yes-or-no issue. When the original question contains だれ・何・いつ・どこ・どう, keep that question word and end the embedded clause with か.

Common mistake

Do not add かどうか after a question word; use いつ来るか, not いつ来るかどうか.

Book examples

Book example

わたしはカンさんに。＋「あしたか」

I asked Mr. Kang. ＋"Can I see you tomorrow?"

→

わたしはカンさんにあしたかどうか（を）。

I asked Mr. Kang if I could meet him tomorrow.

Book example

わたしはわからない。＋「どうすればがになるでしょうか」

I don't know. ＋"How can I become better at Japanese?"

→

わたしはどうすればがになるか（が）わからない。

I don't know how to become good at Japanese.

Added examples

Added example

がにか。

I checked what time the meeting begins.

Why this works

何時 is retained and the embedded question ends in か.

Added example

このがかどうかわかりません。

I do not know whether this answer is correct.

Why this works

かどうか frames the yes-or-no uncertainty.

Book source · pattern 4

どうして／なぜ～かというと、…からだ

Book formation

どうして／なぜ～かというと、…からだ。

Why/Why? It's because...

→３　１

→Part 3 Lesson 1

Expanded explanation

How the sentence is built

This fixed frame first turns the why-question into an embedded clause ending in かというと, then answers it with a reason ending in からだ.

Common mistake

The clause before かというと uses plain form: 人気がある, not 人気があります.

Book examples

Book example

「どうしてこのはがありますか」＋おいしいからです。

"Why is this restaurant so popular?" + Because it's cheap and delicious.

→

どうしてこのががあるかというと、おいしいからです。

The reason why this restaurant is so popular is because the food is cheap and delicious.

Added examples

Added example

なぜをしているかというと、でからです。

Why am I studying Japanese? Because I want to work in Japan.

Why this works

The first half raises the embedded why-question; the second gives the reason.

Added example

どうしてかというと、がからです。

Why was I late? Because the train stopped.

Why this works

Both clauses are plain inside the fixed explanatory frame.
