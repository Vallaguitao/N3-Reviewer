Book overview

「～という…・～といった…」の「～」は「…（）」の・・などを。はっきりのをときは「という・といった」のに「ような」をつけます。

In ～という… and ～といった… clauses, the ～ is a name, description or example (relating to a noun …). To avoid being precise, affix ような after という or といった.

Book source · pattern 1

名前を紹介する（～という…）

Used to give someone or something a name.

Book formation

～という＋

~ + noun

Expanded explanation

How the sentence is built

Place the name immediately before という and then the category noun. In a self-introduction, 者 is a modest way to refer to yourself.

Common mistake

Do not translate という mechanically as “say.” Here it links a name to the person or thing that bears it.

Book examples

Book example

わたしはというです。

My name is Matsushita.

Book example

これはなでしこというです。

This is a flower called dianthus.

Book example

これ、ね。というですか。

This is rare. What kind of fruit is it?

Added examples

Added example

のに「」というカフェがあります。

There is a café called “Aozora” near the station.

Why this works

青空 is the name, and カフェ is the category noun.

Added example

これは「ゆず」というのです。

This is a Japanese fruit called yuzu.

Why this works

The quoted name comes before という and the noun 果物.

Book source · pattern 2

内容を示す（～という…）

Used to convey the purport of a remark, or something heard, etc.

Book formation

＋という＋

ordinary form + called + noun

Expanded explanation

How the sentence is built

A complete plain-form statement comes before という; the noun after it identifies the kind of content, such as a rumor, sound, or meaning.

Common mistake

Keep the embedded clause in plain form. The noun after という is essential because it tells the reader what kind of content is being reported.

Book examples

Book example

がをといううわさを。

I heard a rumor that Mr. Suzuki was leaving the company.

Book example

「ワンワン」というのが。

I can hear the sound of a dog barking "woof woof".

Book example

「」はすぐにあきてしまうというようなだと。

I think "Mikkabozu" means someone who gets bored easily.

Added examples

Added example

がというを。

I received notice that the price will rise next month.

Why this works

The plain statement 来月値段が上がる modifies 知らせ through という.

Added example

で「」というが。

I heard a voice outside saying “Help!”

Why this works

The quoted content identifies what kind of 声 was heard.

Book source · pattern 3

知識・事実などを示す（～ということ）

Used to introduce a statement, or facts about something one has heard or learned.

Book formation

＋ということ

Normal form +

Expanded explanation

How the sentence is built

Turn an entire statement into the noun-like unit ということ so it can be known, heard, learned, considered, or discussed.

Common mistake

Do not confuse ということ with a direct quotation. It packages the preceding proposition as a fact or piece of information.

Book examples

Book example

かぜのにはがいいということをいましたか。

Did you know that washing your hands is a good way to prevent colds?

Book example

のはなだということを。

This is the first time I heard that Makiko's mother is a famous actress.

Book example

いいをのにたくさんのがだということはだと。

I think it's a problem that you need a lot of money to get a good education.

Book example

はこのとき、はというようなことをいた。

When I met my brother the other day, he said something like he was busy this month.

Added examples

Added example

このはにということをください。

Please do not forget that this medicine is taken after meals.

Why this works

The instruction is packaged as the content of what must not be forgotten.

Added example

がにされたということをに。

I told everyone that the meeting had been moved to Friday.

Why this works

ということ marks the fact passed on to everyone.

Book source · pattern 4

よく知らない言葉を示す（～というの）

Used to refer to things that the speaker is not very familiar with.

Book formation

～というのはですか

What is ~?

Expanded explanation

How the sentence is built

Use というの to pick out a term as the topic when asking what it means or what kind of thing it is.

Common mistake

The の nominalizes the unfamiliar expression. Without it, the term cannot naturally serve as the topic before は.

Book examples

Book example

コンピューターで、アップデートというのはですか。

What is an update in computer terminology?

Added examples

Added example

「」というのはどういうですか。

What does “time difference” mean?

Why this works

というの turns the unfamiliar word 時差 into the topic of the question.

Added example

サブスクというのはどんなサービスですか。

What kind of service is a “subscription”?

Why this works

The speaker names a term and asks for its category or meaning.

Book source · pattern 5

いくつかの例を示す（～といった…）

Used to introduce a list of examples.

Book formation

～や～といった＋／～とか～といったような＋

+ noun such as ~ and ~ / + noun such as ~ or ~

Expanded explanation

How the sentence is built

Give representative examples before といった. The following noun states the wider category; ような softens the list further.

Common mistake

The listed items are examples, not a complete list. Use といった when the category continues beyond the named items.

Book examples

Book example

わたしは、、といったのがだ。

I like science subjects such as mathematics, physics, and chemistry.

Book example

はとかといったようなのが。

She looks good in dark colored clothes like black and gray.

Added examples

Added example

やといったをしたいです。

I want to travel to old cities such as Kyoto and Nara.

Why this works

Kyoto and Nara are representative members of the category 古い町.

Added example

やといったにいます。

We are preparing for disasters such as earthquakes and typhoons.

Why this works

The examples lead into the broader noun 災害.
